The Research Goldmine

LinkedIn tells you someone's career history. Twitter tells you what they're thinking about right now. What frustrates them. What excites them. What they're publicly advocating for or against.

A CEO tweeting about supply chain challenges is more receptive to your logistics solution. A VP of Engineering complaining about CI/CD pipelines is primed for your DevOps tool. A CMO sharing thoughts on attribution is ready for that attribution conversation.

Not everyone is on Twitter. But the people who are tend to be influential, public-facing, and willing to engage. Executives at tech companies, venture capitalists, journalists and analysts, startup founders, developers and engineers. These audiences have high Twitter adoption.

If you're selling to them, Twitter profiles are research gold.

What We Provide

  • Verified Twitter handles. Matched to your contact records using bio information, company mentions, and other signals.
  • Profile verification. Common names and ambiguous matches get extra scrutiny. We flag confidence levels so you know which matches are certain.
  • Activity indicators. When someone last tweeted and their approximate posting frequency. Active accounts are more valuable for research than dormant ones.
  • Follower context. Basic follower counts when available. Helps identify influential voices versus casual users.
  • No-match flagging. Clear indication when someone doesn't have a findable Twitter presence. No wasted research time.

Why Twitter Data Matters for Sales

Three ways to use Twitter profiles:

Pre-call research. Scan recent tweets before a meeting. Know what they're focused on. Reference something they posted. Show you did your homework.

Trigger-based outreach. Someone tweets about a problem you solve? That's a warm outreach moment. Better than cold email into the void.

Personalization at scale. Build lists based on what people tweet about. Target accounts where executives are publicly discussing relevant topics. Intent data you can see.
