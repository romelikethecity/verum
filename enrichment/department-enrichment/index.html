The Routing Problem

Lead routing should be simple. Marketing leads go to the marketing-focused rep. Technical leads go to the technical rep. Enterprise accounts get enterprise attention. But routing depends on knowing who you're routing.

Job titles alone don't tell you the department. "Manager" could be anywhere. "Director of Operations" could be IT, could be Sales, could be logistics. And creative titles like "Growth Hacker" or "Evangelist" tell you almost nothing about where they sit in the org chart.

Without department data, your routing rules break. The wrong rep gets the wrong lead. Your messaging is generic because you can't personalize by function. Your conversion rates suffer because prospects hear a pitch designed for someone else.

A data protection company we worked with had 120,000+ contact records with job titles but no standardized departments. Their routing was essentially random. After we mapped all titles to a consistent department taxonomy, their lead-to-meeting conversion improved by 15%. Same leads, better routing, more meetings.
