The Location Data Problem

Territory planning should be straightforward. Assign accounts to reps by geography. Cover the map efficiently. Minimize travel, maximize coverage. Easy enough in theory.

In practice, your location data is a mess. Some accounts show the city where someone filled out a form on their laptop. Others show a Delaware registered agent address when the actual company is in California. Branch offices mixed with headquarters. P.O. boxes instead of street addresses. ZIP codes that don't match the city listed.

The result? Your West Coast rep thinks they own 500 accounts but half of them make buying decisions in New York. Your field team visits offices where the answer is always "You need to talk to headquarters." Your territory reports are fiction because the underlying geography is wrong.

A medical device company we worked with had 120,000+ contact records spread across six states. The address data was chaos. Some records showed abbreviated state names, others spelled them out. Branch offices were listed instead of headquarters. Delaware registered addresses appeared for companies that hadn't operated there in years.

Their territory assignments were essentially guesswork.

What We Provide

  • Headquarters address. The actual decision-making location, not a random office or registered agent. Street address, city, state, ZIP code, and country. Verified and standardized.
  • Branch office locations. For multi-location companies, we identify all known offices. Know where they have presence, not just where they're incorporated.
  • HQ vs. branch distinction. Every address flagged as headquarters, regional office, or branch location. Critical for knowing where to focus your efforts.
  • Format standardization. "California" vs "CA" vs "Calif." all normalized to consistent formats. Your data sorts and filters correctly.
  • ZIP code validation. ZIP codes verified against city and state. We catch the mismatches that break geographic queries.
  • Geographic coordinates. Latitude and longitude for mapping, distance calculations, and routing. Useful for field sales optimization.
