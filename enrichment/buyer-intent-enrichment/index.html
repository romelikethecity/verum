The Timing Problem

You have a list of target accounts. Some of them will buy from you this quarter. Most won't. The challenge is figuring out which is which before your competitors do.

Traditional firmographic data tells you who might be a good fit. Intent data tells you who's actively looking right now. Company size and industry don't change week to week. Buying behavior does.

Without intent signals, you're treating all accounts the same. Equal outreach to companies actively evaluating vendors and companies that won't think about your category for another year. Same campaigns, same cadences, same wasted effort on accounts that aren't ready.

The companies that get there first often win. Not because their product is better, but because they showed up when the buyer was actively looking.

What Intent Signals Tell You

Intent data captures digital behavior that suggests buying interest. When someone at a company reads articles about your category, visits competitor websites, searches for related keywords, or downloads relevant content, those actions create signals.

No single signal means much. Someone reading one article isn't a buyer. But when multiple people at the same company show sustained interest over weeks, that's a pattern worth acting on.

We aggregate signals from content publishers, review sites like G2 and TrustRadius, search behavior data, and specialized intent providers. Multiple sources, multiple signal types, scored together for reliability.

Common Questions

What is buyer intent data?

Buyer intent data tracks which companies are actively researching topics related to your product. It's based on content consumption, search behavior, and engagement patterns that signal a company is in-market.

We aggregate signals from content publishers, review sites, search patterns, and third-party intent providers. Multiple signals create more reliable intent scores.

How fresh is the intent data?

Intent signals are time-sensitive. We provide data current within 30-90 days, depending on signal type. Older intent data loses predictive value quickly.
