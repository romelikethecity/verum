Why NAICS Codes Matter

Industry data is supposed to help you segment and target. But when your industry field is freeform text entered by different people at different times from different sources, it doesn't help much.

You want to target healthcare companies. So you filter by "Healthcare." But you miss "Medical," "Health Services," "Hospital," "Pharma," "Life Sciences," and the dozen other variations people used. Some healthcare companies are labeled "Technology" because they make medical software. Your segment is incomplete.

NAICS codes solve this. They're the official US government classification system. Every industry has a specific code. Healthcare and Social Assistance is sector 62. Hospitals specifically are 622. General Medical and Surgical Hospitals are 622110. When you filter by code, you catch everything.

Common Questions

What is a NAICS code?

North American Industry Classification System. The standard used by US, Canada, and Mexico to classify businesses. 6-digit hierarchical codes from broad sector to specific industry.

Why use NAICS instead of freeform industry fields?

Standardization. "Tech" and "Technology" and "Software" mean different things to different people. NAICS 541511 always means the same thing. Your filters work.

Do you provide the full 6-digit code?

Yes. Full 6-digit plus all parent codes (2-digit sector through 5-digit industry). Filter at whatever level you need.

What about companies in multiple industries?

We assign primary NAICS based on main revenue activity. Secondary codes available when a company has significant operations across industries.
