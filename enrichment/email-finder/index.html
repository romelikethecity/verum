The Email Gap

You know who you want to reach. Name, company, title. Maybe you scraped a list from an event. Maybe you built a target account list with specific contacts. What you don't have is their email address.

Pattern guessing is unreliable. Yes, many companies use firstname.lastname@company.com. But many use first.last@, flastname@, or completely different patterns. And even when you guess the right pattern, you don't know if that specific mailbox exists. The CEO might be ceo@ while everyone else is firstname.lastname@. You're sending emails into the void hoping some land.

We find emails by actually looking them up. Cross-referencing multiple sources, verifying the mailbox exists, and confirming the person still works there. Not guessing. Finding.

A data protection company we worked with had a list of target contacts but email addresses for only about half. Their SDRs were spending hours researching each contact before outreach. After we found verified emails for the remaining contacts, that research time disappeared. Same targets, faster outreach, more conversations.
