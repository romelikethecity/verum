The Wrong-Contact Problem

Your CRM is full of contacts. Marketing captured them from webinars, content downloads, trade shows. But how many of them can actually buy? Not influence. Not evaluate. Actually sign a purchase order.

Most contacts in most CRMs are influencers at best. Individual contributors who might use your product. Managers who might recommend it. But when it comes to budget authority, they'll tell you "I need to run this by my VP" or "Let me check with finance." The real decision maker isn't in your database.

Selling to the wrong person is worse than not selling at all. You invest time, resources, and pipeline capacity into deals that stall at the budget conversation. Your champion loves you but can't move the organization. Meanwhile, the actual decision maker doesn't know you exist.

The solution isn't more contacts. It's the right contacts. People with budget authority for what you sell, at the companies you're targeting, with contact information that lets you reach them directly.

What We Provide

  • Decision maker identification. Not just title matching. We analyze seniority, department, company structure, and budget patterns to find who actually controls purchasing decisions for your category.
  • Purchase-type targeting. The decision maker for software is different from the decision maker for services. Tell us what you sell, we'll find who buys it.
  • Full contact details. Verified email, direct dial, mobile when available, LinkedIn profile. Multiple channels to reach them.
  • Buying committee mapping. For complex sales, you need the committee, not just one person. We identify economic buyers, technical evaluators, and executive sponsors when relevant.
  • Company context. How big is the company? How is it structured? Who reports to whom? Context that helps you navigate the organization.
  • Budget authority indicators. Signals that suggest this person controls spend: their title relative to company size, their tenure, their department's role in purchasing decisions.
