The Budget Problem

You're reaching out to accounts that look like they should be good fits. Right industry. Right size. Right role. But the deals stall. Turns out they're not buying anything this year because they're conserving cash. Or the budget conversation never happens because there is no budget.

Funding data changes that. A company that just raised $50M in Series B isn't conserving cash. They're spending it. Aggressively. They have investors expecting growth, board members asking about infrastructure, and a mandate to scale. Your product might be exactly what they need to get there.

The timing matters too. The best window to reach a recently-funded company is the first few months after the round closes. That's when they're making vendor decisions. That's when the budget is allocated. Wait six months and the money's already committed elsewhere.

Without funding data, you're flying blind on which accounts have budget and which are just browsing. You're treating the Series C company the same as the company that hasn't raised in five years and is running lean. Same effort, very different close rates.

What We Provide

  • Funding round type. Seed, Series A through E, growth equity, debt financing. The round type tells you where they are in their journey and what they're likely prioritizing.
  • Amount raised. Exact dollar amounts when disclosed, ranges when not. A $5M seed is different from a $100M Series C. The amount tells you the scale of spending to expect.
  • Funding date. When the round closed. Recent funding (last 6 months) signals active spending. Older rounds suggest the budget may already be committed.
  • Lead investors. Which firms led the round. Some investors push aggressive tool purchases. Others encourage bootstrapped efficiency. The investor often shapes the spending culture.
  • Total funding to date. Cumulative capital raised across all rounds. Context for understanding the company's overall financial position.
  • Valuation (when available). Post-money valuation for rounds where it's disclosed. Useful for understanding company stage and expectations.
