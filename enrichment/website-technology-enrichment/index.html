What Websites Reveal

Websites are remarkably chatty about their underlying technology. Open source in the browser and you see the story. Google Analytics tracking code. HubSpot form handlers. WordPress meta tags. Shopify checkout signatures. CDN headers. The evidence is everywhere.

This isn't hacking or scraping proprietary data. It's reading what companies publicly broadcast through their websites. Every tracking script, every embedded widget, every framework signature tells you something about their technology stack.

We systematically decode these signals across hundreds of technologies. The result: a detailed profile of what each company uses for analytics, marketing, content management, e-commerce, and more.

How Detection Works

We scan each website looking for technology signatures:

Script analysis. JavaScript files and tracking pixels with identifiable patterns. ga('create', 'UA-...' tells us Google Analytics Universal. gtag() tells us GA4.

DOM inspection. HTML elements, class names, and data attributes often reveal the tools that generated them. WordPress themes have recognizable patterns. Shopify checkouts have consistent signatures.

Header analysis. HTTP headers reveal CDNs, server software, and caching layers. X-Powered-By, Server, and custom headers provide signals.

Multiple page scanning. Homepage alone doesn't tell the whole story. We check key pages to catch technologies only used in specific contexts.
