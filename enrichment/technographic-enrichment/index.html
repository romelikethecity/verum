Beyond Company Size and Industry

Traditional segmentation uses firmographics: industry, company size, revenue, location. Useful, but incomplete. Two 500-person SaaS companies might have completely different technology needs based on their current stack.

Technographics adds the technology dimension. What CRM do they use? What marketing automation? What analytics platform? What cloud infrastructure? This context changes everything about how you sell.

If you sell a Salesforce integration, you need to know who uses Salesforce. If you compete with HubSpot, you need to know who's running HubSpot. If your product requires AWS, you need to know who's on AWS. Technographics makes this targeting possible at scale.

Common Questions

What's the difference between technographics and tech stack data?

Tech stack is what they use. Technographics is broader — includes tech stack plus adoption patterns, sophistication indicators, and investment signals.

How do technographics help with sales?

Qualification, positioning, prioritization. Know if they use your competitor, have complementary tools, or are technically sophisticated. Better targeting, higher conversion.

Can you tell when companies are evaluating new tech?

Sometimes. Job postings, trial activity, new tracking scripts can signal evaluation. Combined with technographics, these intent signals improve timing.

How often does technographic data change?

Tech stacks evolve. Website changes show up quickly, backend changes take longer. We recommend quarterly refreshes for active sales campaigns.
