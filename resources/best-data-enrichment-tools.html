10 Best Data Enrichment Tools for B2B

A practical comparison of the leading platforms. What they do well, where they fall short, and what they actually cost.

January 2026 · 15 min read

You need to enrich your CRM data. Missing phone numbers, incomplete company information, outdated job titles. The question is: which tool should you use?

The market has dozens of options, from enterprise platforms costing $50K+ per year to credit-based tools where you pay per record. Each has different strengths: some excel at North American data, others focus on Europe. Some are best for prospecting, others for enriching existing records.

This guide breaks down the major players so you can find the right fit for your team and budget.

The Tools, In Depth

1. ZoomInfo

Enterprise

The market leader with the largest North American B2B database. Best for enterprise sales teams with budget to match.

Pricing $15,000-$50,000+/year
Database Size 200M+ contacts, 14M+ companies
Best For North American enterprise sales
Integrations Salesforce, HubSpot, Outreach, etc.
Strengths
  • Largest US B2B database
  • Strong company hierarchies
  • Intent data included (higher tiers)
  • Robust integrations
Weaknesses
  • Expensive, annual contracts
  • Weaker outside North America
  • Feature bloat for simple use cases
  • Mobile numbers less reliable

2. Cognism

Enterprise

The go-to for European data with GDPR compliance baked in. Known for phone-verified mobile numbers.

Pricing $15,000-$40,000+/year
Database Size 400M+ profiles globally
Best For EMEA-focused teams, phone outreach
Integrations Salesforce, HubSpot, Outreach
Strengths
  • Best European data coverage
  • Phone-verified mobile numbers
  • Strong GDPR compliance
  • Good intent data
Weaknesses
  • Weaker in North America vs ZoomInfo
  • Annual contracts required

How to Choose the Right Tool

Consider Your Primary Use Case

Prospecting (finding new contacts): ZoomInfo, Apollo, or Cognism give you searchable databases to build lists from scratch.

Enriching existing records: Clearbit or Clay work well for appending data to records you already have.

Phone-first outreach: Cognism or Lusha offer the best phone number data, including mobile verification.

ABM and intent: 6sense or Demandbase provide intent signals to identify in-market accounts.

Consider Your Budget

Under $5,000/year: Apollo, Lusha, or Seamless.AI. You'll get decent data with some accuracy trade-offs.

$15,000-$50,000/year: ZoomInfo, Cognism, or Clearbit. Better data quality, more features, dedicated support.

$50,000+/year: 6sense, Demandbase, or ZoomInfo Enterprise. Full platforms with intent, orchestration, and analytics.

Consider Your Geography

North America: ZoomInfo or Apollo offer the deepest coverage.

Europe: Cognism is the clear leader for EMEA data with GDPR compliance.

Global: You may need multiple tools or a service that aggregates sources.

An Alternative: Data Enrichment Services

Tools require ongoing subscriptions and someone to run them. If you don't need continuous access to a prospecting database, a one-time enrichment service might be more cost-effective.

Enrichment services take your existing records, clean them, and enrich them from multiple sources. You pay per record (typically $0.50-$2.00) instead of an annual subscription. Better for periodic database maintenance than ongoing prospecting.

Common Questions

What is the best data enrichment tool for B2B?

It depends on your needs. ZoomInfo offers the largest North American database but costs $15K-50K+/year. Apollo provides good value for SMBs. Cognism excels at European data. Clay offers flexibility for custom workflows.

How much do data enrichment tools cost?

Enterprise platforms like ZoomInfo and Cognism cost $15,000-$50,000+ per year. Mid-market tools like Apollo and Lusha range from $49-$99 per user per month. Credit-based tools charge per enrichment, usually $0.10-$1.00 per record.

What's the difference between data enrichment tools and services?

Tools are platforms you use yourself. Services are done-for-you offerings. Tools require ongoing subscriptions and internal resources. Services are typically one-time projects with fixed per-record pricing.
