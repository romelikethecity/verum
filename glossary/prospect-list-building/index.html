Prospect List Building

Prospect list building is the process of creating a targeted list of companies and contacts that match your ideal customer profile for sales outreach. It combines firmographic filtering (industry, size, location), contact identification (finding the right people at each company), and data verification (confirming emails deliver and phone numbers connect). A good prospect list is the starting point for every outbound sales motion.

Why It Matters

Sales reps should spend their time selling, not researching. When reps build their own lists by manually searching LinkedIn, company websites, and Google, they spend 30-40% of their day on research instead of outreach. And the lists they build are inconsistent, each rep has different standards for what counts as a good prospect. Centralized, data-driven list building gives every rep a consistent, verified starting point so they can focus on conversations.

How to Build a Quality Prospect List

  • Define your ICP criteria: Company size, industry, geography, tech stack, growth signals, and any other attributes that predict a good customer
  • Source company data: Use firmographic databases to find all companies matching your criteria
  • Identify contacts: Find the right people at each company: decision-makers, influencers, and budget holders with the titles you target
  • Verify contact data: Run email verification and phone validation. A list with 30% bad data is worse than a smaller clean list
  • Enrich for personalization: Append tech stack, funding data, hiring trends, and other fields that enable relevant outreach

Example

A company needs to prospect into mid-market healthcare organizations. They define the ICP: 200-2000 employees, healthcare or health-tech industry, US-based, using Salesforce. The list build returns 1,400 companies with 4,200 verified contacts (VP+ in Operations, IT, and Finance). Each contact has email, phone, LinkedIn URL, and company firmographics. Reps start calling the same day.
