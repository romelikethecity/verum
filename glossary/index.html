Data Quality Glossary

Key terms in B2B data quality, CRM management, and data enrichment.

Data Enrichment

The process of appending missing information to existing records using external data sources. Adds emails, phone numbers, job titles, company size, and other fields to CRM records.

Data Decay

The rate at which business data becomes inaccurate over time. B2B data decays at roughly 30% per year due to job changes, company closures, and acquisitions.

Firmographic Data

Descriptive attributes of a business, including company size, industry, revenue, location, and founding date. The B2B equivalent of demographics for individual consumers.

Data Deduplication

The process of identifying and merging duplicate records in a database. Most CRM databases contain 10-25% duplicate records that waste sales time and distort reporting.

Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

A data-driven description of the companies most likely to become your best customers, based on analysis of firmographic, technographic, and behavioral attributes of existing accounts.

Contact Data Waterfall

A sequential enrichment strategy that queries multiple data providers in order, using each source's strengths to maximize coverage while minimizing cost.

CRM Hygiene

The ongoing practice of maintaining clean, accurate, and complete data in a customer relationship management system through regular deduplication, validation, and enrichment.

Technographic Data

Information about the technology stack a company uses, including CRM, marketing automation, e-commerce platform, analytics tools, and other software.

Email Validation

The process of verifying that email addresses are valid, deliverable, and belong to real inboxes. Reduces bounce rates and protects sender reputation.

Data Normalization

Standardizing data formats and values so records can be accurately compared, deduplicated, and analyzed. Includes job title standardization, company name normalization, and address formatting.
