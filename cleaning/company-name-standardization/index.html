The Company Name Problem

Company names are entered by humans. Humans are inconsistent. One rep types "Acme Corp." Another types "Acme Corporation." A third imports a list with "ACME CO." Now you have three records for one company.

This isn't just a cosmetic issue. Fragmented records mean fragmented customer history. Your big account looks like three small accounts. Revenue attribution is wrong. Your top-accounts report is inaccurate. The VP asks "how much business do we do with Acme?" and nobody can answer confidently.

Company name standardization brings all variations to one consistent format. Now deduplication works. Now reporting is accurate. Now you can actually see your customer relationships clearly.

What We Standardize

  • Legal suffix normalization. Inc, Inc., Incorporated, Corp, Corp., Corporation, Co, Co., Company, LLC, L.L.C., Ltd, Limited. All brought to your preferred format.
  • Punctuation cleanup. Periods, commas, extra spaces, inconsistent capitalization. Everything cleaned to a consistent style.
  • Abbreviation handling. "Int'l" becomes "International." "Mfg" becomes "Manufacturing." Common abbreviations expanded or standardized.
  • Typo correction. Common misspellings caught and fixed. "Microsft" becomes "Microsoft." "Amazo" becomes "Amazon."
  • DBA/trading name alignment. Some records use legal names, others use trading names. We note the relationship and standardize consistently.
  • Capitalization standardization. "ACME CORPORATION" and "acme corporation" both become "Acme Corporation."

Why Standardization Enables Everything Else

Standardization is foundation work. Until company names are consistent, other data operations fail.

Deduplication. You can't find duplicates if the same company appears as three different text strings. Standardization makes matching possible.

Data enrichment. Appending data from external sources requires matching. "Acme Corp" might not match their database's "Acme Corporation."

Reporting. Roll-up reports, territory analysis, account tiering. All depend on consistent names to group records correctly.

Integration. Syncing between systems fails when names don't match. Standardization enables clean integration.

Common Questions

What variations do you standardize?

Legal suffixes (Inc, Corp, LLC, Ltd), punctuation, spacing, capitalization, abbreviations, typos, and DBA names.

What about subsidiaries and parent companies?

That's a separate service (parent-subsidiary mapping). Standardization focuses on making the name itself consistent.

What about multiple legal names?

Some companies have multiple legitimate names. We note when this occurs while standardizing to your chosen format.

Do you preserve original names?

Yes. We add a standardized field rather than overwriting. You keep your original data for reference.
