The Accumulated Mess

Databases don't start messy. They get that way over time.

Year one is fine. Year two, a few problems creep in. By year five, you've got duplicates everywhere, thousands of bouncing emails, formatting inconsistencies that break every report, and records that might have been valid once but aren't anymore.

How does it get this bad? List imports without proper deduplication. Reps entering data inconsistently. Contacts changing jobs but records staying frozen. Systems syncing the same person with slightly different information. Every year adds another layer of mess on top of the last one.

Eventually, trust in the data collapses. Teams start maintaining their own spreadsheets. Campaigns go out to people who haven't worked at those companies for years. Reporting shows numbers nobody believes. The database becomes a liability instead of an asset.

A full cleanup resets everything. Address all the problems at once, get back to clean, and then maintain it going forward.

What Full Cleanup Includes

  • Duplicate detection and merge. Find duplicates by email, by name + company, by phone. Exact matches and fuzzy matches. Merge records keeping the best data from each.
  • Email validation. Every email verified against mail servers. Bounces, spam traps, and invalid addresses identified before they hurt deliverability.
  • Phone standardization. Consistent formatting across all phone numbers. Country codes added where missing. Invalid numbers flagged.
  • Address normalization. Street, city, state, ZIP in consistent formats. Validation against postal databases. Undeliverable addresses flagged.
  • Field normalization. Job titles, company names, industries, states, countries. All brought to consistent formats.
  • Completeness analysis. Which records are missing critical fields? What percentage of your database is actually usable? Full visibility into data quality.
  • Invalid record removal. Clearly bad records separated out. You decide what to delete, keep, or investigate further.
