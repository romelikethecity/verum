The Duplicate Problem

Duplicates creep in everywhere. Marketing imports a list, sales manually enters the same contact. Different systems sync the same person with slightly different data. Name variations create records that look different but aren't.

The consequences compound over time. Your biggest account looks like five separate small accounts. Customer history is fragmented across multiple records. Marketing emails the same person three times. Territory assignments break. Revenue attribution is wrong.

The worst part: simple duplicate detection misses most of them. "john.smith@acme.com" is easy to match. "John Smith at Acme Corp" vs "Jon Smith at Acme Corporation" requires fuzzy matching. Most tools only do exact matching. We do both.

How We Find Duplicates

  • Exact matching. Same email, same phone number, same unique ID. The obvious duplicates.
  • Fuzzy name matching. "John Smith" vs "Jon Smith" vs "J. Smith." Nickname variations, typos, missing middle names. We catch them.
  • Company name matching. "Acme Corporation" vs "Acme Corp" vs "ACME CO." Same company, different text.
  • Multi-field matching. Name + company, name + domain, phone + company. Multiple signals combined for confident matching.
  • Cross-object matching. Leads, contacts, accounts. Find the same entity across different CRM objects.
  • Confidence scoring. Not all matches are equal. High-confidence matches merge automatically. Lower confidence gets flagged for review.

How We Merge

Finding duplicates is half the problem. Merging them properly is the other half.

Best data preservation. When merging two records, we keep the best data from each. Most recent email, most complete phone, most detailed address. Nothing valuable is lost.

History consolidation. Activities, opportunities, notes. All associated data follows to the surviving record.

Audit trail. Documentation of what was merged and why. You can see exactly what changed.

Rollback capability. Original records preserved until you confirm the merge is correct.

Common Questions

What's exact vs fuzzy matching?

Exact finds identical values (same email). Fuzzy finds probable matches despite differences ("John Smith" vs "Jon Smith"). You need both.

How do you decide which record to keep?

We merge to create one record with the best data from each duplicate. Most recent email, most complete phone, richest combined record.

What's a typical duplicate rate?

10-30% is common. We've seen 40%+ in databases with years of list imports without proper matching.

Can you handle cross-object duplicates?

Yes. Same person as Lead, Contact, and in multiple Accounts. We find them and provide consolidation recommendations.
