The Format Chaos

Your database has the same information represented a dozen different ways. Not wrong information, just inconsistently formatted. And that inconsistency breaks things.

Phone numbers are the obvious example. (555) 123-4567. 555-123-4567. 555.123.4567. 5551234567. +1 555 123 4567. 1-555-123-4567. All the same number. All completely different text strings. Your dialer can handle maybe half of these. Your deduplication catches none of the matches.

Dates have the same problem, but worse because the inconsistency can change meaning. 01/02/2024. Is that January 2nd or February 1st? Depends on who entered it. If you have international data, you have both interpretations mixed together with no way to know which is which.

Company names. Acme Inc. Acme Inc without the period. Acme Incorporated. ACME. Same company, different text. Your account-based reporting shows four separate records.

States and countries. California, CA, Cal. United States, US, USA, U.S.A. Your territory mapping relies on exact matches. Half your records don't match.

Every field where humans type instead of select from a dropdown accumulates this chaos over time.

What We Standardize

  • Phone numbers. Consistent format throughout. E.164 international standard, or your preferred domestic format. Country codes included or excluded as needed.
  • Dates. ISO format (2024-01-15) or your preferred pattern. Ambiguous dates resolved through context and source analysis.
  • Addresses. Street types (St/Street/Str), directional prefixes (N/North), unit designations (Apt/Suite/#). All unified.
  • Company names. Legal suffixes (Inc/Incorporated/Corp/Corporation), punctuation, capitalization. Consistent format for matching and reporting.
  • Geographic fields. State abbreviations vs full names. Country codes vs full names. Your choice of standard, applied consistently.
  • Industry classifications. Free-text industries mapped to standard codes (NAICS, SIC) or your custom taxonomy.
  • Job titles. Variations mapped to standard categories. VP/Vice President, Dir/Director, Mgr/Manager.

Why Standardization Enables Everything Else

Standardization is foundation work. Until formats are consistent, other data operations fail.

Deduplication requires matching. The same value in different formats won't match. Standardize first, then deduplicate.

Reporting requires grouping. "CA" and "California" need to be the same value to appear in the same bucket. Standardization makes rollups accurate.

Integration requires compatibility. Systems exchange data in specific formats. If your data doesn't match, sync fails. Standardization enables clean integration.

Automation requires predictability. Lead routing, segmentation, scoring. All depend on consistent values. Variations break rules.

Common Questions

What fields can be standardized?

Any field with formatting variations: phones, dates, addresses, company names, states, countries, job titles, industries. If humans entered it, it probably needs standardization.

Can we choose our own formats?

Yes. E.164 phones or simpler domestic format. ISO dates or MM/DD/YYYY. We standardize to your chosen format.

How is standardization different from cleaning?

Standardization is about format consistency. Cleaning is broader: duplicates, validation, missing data. Standardization is one component of full cleaning.

Will this help with deduplication?

Absolutely. Deduplication depends on matching. Same values in different formats don't match. Standardize first, then deduplicate.
