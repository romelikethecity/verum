The Decay Reality

Email addresses die constantly. People change jobs, get promoted, move companies. Email domains get acquired or shut down. What was a valid address six months ago might bounce today.

Industry data suggests about 30% of B2B email addresses become invalid within a year. That's nearly a third of your list going bad annually. A database you haven't touched in two years might have more dead addresses than live ones.

The cost isn't just wasted sends. High bounce rates damage your sender reputation. Email providers watch how often your messages bounce. Cross a threshold and they start routing you to spam, or blocking you entirely. One bad campaign to an unverified list can hurt deliverability for months.

Verification catches the dead addresses before they become bounces. Know what's valid before you send.

How Verification Works

Real email verification checks whether a mailbox exists, not just whether an address looks valid.

  • Domain check. Does the domain exist and resolve? Catches addresses at defunct or fake domains.
  • MX record lookup. Does the domain have mail servers configured? Some domains exist but don't receive email.
  • SMTP connection. Can we connect to the mail server? Confirms the infrastructure is working.
  • Mailbox verification. Does this specific mailbox exist? We ask the server directly without sending a message.
  • Response analysis. What does the server tell us? "User unknown" means invalid. "OK" means valid. Other responses need interpretation.

The Catch-All Challenge

Some organizations configure their mail servers as "catch-all." These servers accept all mail regardless of whether the specific mailbox exists. The mail goes to a central inbox or gets discarded later, but the server doesn't reject it during SMTP verification.

This makes verification uncertain for those domains. The server says "yes" to every address, even fake ones. We identify catch-all domains and flag those addresses separately so you know which results are definitive and which are uncertain.

Microsoft 365 and some Google Workspace configurations also block verification requests, returning inconclusive results. We handle these carefully, using additional signals when available.

Common Questions

Validation vs verification?

Often interchangeable. Technically, validation checks format; verification confirms existence. Good email checking does both.

How does SMTP verification work?

We connect to the mail server and ask if the mailbox exists, then disconnect before actually sending. Confirms deliverability without test emails.

What about catch-all domains?

They accept all mail regardless of mailbox existence. We can't definitively verify these addresses, so we flag them separately.

How accurate is verification?

93%+ on most lists. Some providers block verification requests, making those addresses harder to confirm. We mark uncertain results clearly.
