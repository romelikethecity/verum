Account Management Without Data Is Just Guessing

Renewal is coming up. You need to know: Is the champion still there? Has the company grown? Are they using competitive products? Who else should you be talking to? One hour of prep, digging through LinkedIn and Google, and you still don't have the full picture.

Champions leave without warning. About 30% of B2B contacts change jobs every year. Your CRM still shows them. The renewal email bounces. You find out too late that your advocate left six months ago and nobody's been nurturing the replacement.

Accounts are deeper than you think. You sold to marketing. IT is frustrated they weren't consulted. Finance has budget questions nobody anticipated. The org chart in your CRM has five people. The actual buying committee has fifteen.

Growth signals hide in plain sight. Your customer just raised a Series C. They're hiring across every department. Opening three new offices. Perfect expansion opportunity, except nobody told account management. They found out when the customer called to ask about enterprise pricing.

Multi-location complexity compounds. You sold to HQ. The UK office went with a competitor. The APAC team is evaluating options. Same company, fragmented relationship, no visibility into the full footprint.
