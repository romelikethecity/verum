The Revenue Intelligence Problem

You've invested in revenue intelligence. Dashboards showing ARR by segment. Churn analysis by cohort. Win rate trends by industry. The reports look impressive.

But the underlying data is a mess. Industry classification is blank for 30% of customers. Company size is whatever someone entered when the deal closed, not what it is today. Half your "enterprise" customers are actually mid-market because the original rep wanted a bigger deal classification.

So your revenue intelligence is telling you stories that aren't true. You think healthcare is your fastest-growing segment, but it might just be the segment with the most complete industry data. You think enterprise customers have higher LTV, but your enterprise segment includes companies that aren't actually enterprise.

A B2B platform we analyzed had exactly this problem. They had revenue reports by segment, but when we examined their 315 closed deals, we found the segments they were tracking weren't the segments that actually mattered.

What the data revealed

Company size and industry, their two primary segmentation dimensions, were weak predictors of revenue. The real patterns were hiding in attributes they'd never tracked.

RevOps team structure predicted lifetime value better than anything else. Companies with exactly two RevOps people had $8,750 LTV. Companies with one RevOps person had $2,100 LTV. Same company size. Same industry. Four times the revenue difference.

CRM platform predicted win rates dramatically. Dynamics CRM users closed at 99%. Pipedrive users closed at 7%. Their revenue reports by industry were showing noise. Revenue reports by CRM platform would have shown signal.

We also identified $2 million per year in preventable churn. Customers with three or more RevOps people churned at 55%. Customers using multiple CRMs churned at 58%. These weren't random losses. These were predictable losses they could have prevented if they'd known to watch for these patterns.
