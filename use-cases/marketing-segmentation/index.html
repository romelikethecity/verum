The Marketing Segmentation Problem

You've built segments in your marketing automation platform. Enterprise versus mid-market versus SMB. Maybe industry verticals. Perhaps a few behavioral segments based on engagement.

The problem is your underlying data. Industry is blank for a third of your contacts. Company size comes from when someone filled out a form three years ago. Your "enterprise" segment includes companies with 50 employees because someone checked the wrong box.

So your "personalized" campaigns go to the wrong people. Enterprise messaging lands in mid-market inboxes. Industry-specific content reaches accounts in completely different verticals. Your engagement rates suffer because the targeting is off.

A B2B platform we worked with had exactly this challenge. They had 315 closed deals to analyze, customers across multiple segments, and a vague sense that some customer types were better than others. They couldn't tell which ones.

What we discovered

We analyzed their data across five dimensions: company growth velocity, SDR team size, CRM platform, geographic distribution, and RevOps team maturity. Standard stuff. What wasn't standard was what we found.

RevOps team size was the strongest predictor of customer success. Not company size. Not industry. Not funding stage. Companies with exactly two RevOps people had an $8,750 lifetime value and 62% win rates. Companies with one RevOps person had $2,100 LTV but 68% win rates. Companies with three or more? 55% churn.

This wasn't a segment anyone had considered. "Number of RevOps people" isn't a standard firmographic field. But it was the most predictive attribute in their entire database.

We also found CRM platform mattered enormously. Dynamics CRM users had a 99% win rate. Pipedrive users had 7%. That's not a small difference. That's the difference between a viable market and a money pit.

  • Custom attributes. If it exists in public data, we can likely find it. Job title patterns, hiring activity, technology adoption signals.
