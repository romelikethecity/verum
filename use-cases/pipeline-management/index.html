Why Pipeline Data Matters

Pipeline review meetings shouldn't be archaeology expeditions. But that's what happens when data quality degrades. Reps dig through contacts to figure out who's still at the company. Managers ask why the same account appears twice. Leadership wonders why forecasts are always off.

Duplicate accounts inflate everything. The same company shows up as three opportunities because it's in the CRM three ways. Pipeline looks bigger than it is. Coverage ratios are wrong. Forecasts are optimistic until they're not.

Stale contacts hide dead deals. Your champion left four months ago. Nobody updated the record. The opportunity sits in pipeline, forecasted to close, attached to someone who doesn't work there. Research shows 30% of contacts change jobs every year. Your pipeline is full of ghosts.

Missing data prevents prioritization. You don't know which deals are enterprise vs. mid-market because company size isn't filled in. Can't prioritize by vertical because industry is blank. Reps guess instead of knowing where to focus.

Bad data compounds. One wrong field leads to another. Automations fire incorrectly. Routing rules break. Marketing scores based on incomplete info. By the time a deal reaches late stage, nobody trusts any of the data attached to it.

Clean pipeline data means accurate forecasts, better prioritization, and less time wasted on deals that aren't real.

Common Questions

How does data quality affect forecasting?

Directly. Bad data means inflated pipeline (duplicates), false optimism (stale deals), and poor prioritization (missing firmographics). Clean data enables accurate forecasts.

What pipeline data do you clean?

Account and contact data primarily. We verify contacts, standardize companies, merge duplicates, and fill firmographic gaps. We also flag stale opportunities.

Can you identify stale deals?

Yes. We check if contacts are still at the company. If your champion left, we flag it. About 30% of contacts change jobs yearly.

How often should we clean pipeline?

Quarterly minimum. Monthly for high-velocity teams. One-time deep clean plus ongoing maintenance works well.

What about historical pipeline?

We can clean current pipeline, historical closed-won for analysis, or both. Historical data cleaning helps with win/loss analysis and forecasting model training.
