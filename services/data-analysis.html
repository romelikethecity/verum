What Data Analysis Actually Looks Like

A Series A B2B platform came to us trying to figure out where to spend their marketing budget. They had 315 closed deals, customers across multiple segments, and a vague sense that some customer types were better than others. They didn't know which ones.

We analyzed their data across five dimensions: company growth velocity, SDR team size, CRM platform, geographic distribution, and RevOps team maturity. Standard stuff. What wasn't standard was what we found.

The key finding

RevOps team size was the strongest predictor of customer success. Not company size. Not industry. Not funding stage. The number of dedicated RevOps professionals.

Companies with exactly two RevOps people had an $8,750 lifetime value, 25% churn, and 62% win rates. Companies with one RevOps person had $2,100 LTV but a 68% win rate and only 22% churn. Companies with three or more RevOps? 55% churn and 50% win rates. Not ready for enterprise yet.

This wasn't intuitive. Nobody on their team would have guessed that "2 RevOps" was the magic number. But the data was clear.

What we also discovered

Dynamics CRM users had a 99% win rate. Ninety-nine percent. The market was severely underserved. Nobody was selling to them effectively.

Pipedrive users had a 7% win rate. Fatal. Every dollar spent acquiring Pipedrive users was essentially wasted.

Dual-CRM setups (HubSpot for marketing, Salesforce for sales) had 58% churn. Integration complexity was killing retention.

We identified $2M per year in preventable churn just by better qualifying which customers to pursue.

The recommendations

40% of marketing budget to the "2 RevOps" segment. 35% to the "1 RevOps" growth flywheel. Those customers were easy to acquire and naturally expanded into the higher-LTV segment. 10% to experiment with Dynamics users. 15% to retention programs.

We didn't just tell them what was happening. We told them what to do about it.

Common Questions

How much data do you need?

Ideally 6+ months of customer data and 100+ closed deals. We can work with less, but the insights will be directional rather than statistically significant. We'll tell you upfront what's possible with your dataset.

What if our data is messy?

Most data is. We often recommend starting with data cleaning before analysis. Garbage in, garbage out applies here. We can do both as part of a single engagement.

How long does analysis take?

ICP analysis typically takes 2-3 weeks from data receipt to final deliverable. More complex analyses (churn modeling, multi-segment strategies) may take 4-6 weeks. We'll scope it clearly upfront.

What's the difference between this and a BI tool?

BI tools show you what happened. We tell you what to do about it. You get strategic recommendations, not just dashboards. Budget allocation. Targeting criteria. Specific segments to pursue or avoid. Actionable insights, not pretty charts.
