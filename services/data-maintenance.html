Ongoing data maintenance is a continuous process of keeping business databases accurate and current through regular validation, enrichment refreshes, new prospect discovery, and periodic deep cleans. B2B data decays at approximately 30% per year as contacts change jobs, companies get acquired, phone numbers disconnect, and email addresses bounce. Without scheduled maintenance, even a freshly cleaned database becomes unreliable within 6-12 months.

Data Quality Is Not a One-Time Project

You cleaned your database. Great. But B2B data decays 30% per year. Contacts change jobs. Businesses close. Phone numbers disconnect. The database you cleaned six months ago is already getting stale.

Most companies treat data quality as an occasional project. Clean it once, forget about it, then panic when bounce rates spike or sales reps start complaining again.

There's a better way: continuous maintenance that keeps your data fresh without requiring constant attention from your team.

Common Questions

Do I need to clean my database first?

Yes. Ongoing maintenance keeps clean data fresh. It doesn't fix years of accumulated mess. We typically start with a one-time cleaning project, then transition to ongoing maintenance.

What format do I get the data in?

Your choice: Excel files, Google Sheets, or direct push to your CRM (Salesforce, HubSpot). We work with whatever fits your workflow.

Can I pause the service?

Yes. Life happens. You can pause maintenance and restart when you're ready. We don't lock you into long-term contracts.

How do you handle data security?

Same as our project work: encrypted transfers, no sharing with third parties, deletion on request. Happy to sign NDAs or discuss specific compliance requirements.

What if my market doesn't have many new businesses?

Then you probably don't need weekly new prospect alerts. We can customize the package to focus on validation and change monitoring instead of discovery.
