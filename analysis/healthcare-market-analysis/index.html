Healthcare Market Analysis

Healthcare market analysis provides a data-driven view of the healthcare provider landscape: hospitals, practices, clinics, and provider groups by specialty, size, geography, and ownership structure. We use provider databases, licensing data, and business registrations to build accurate market maps.

Healthcare is one of the largest markets in the US, but it's also one of the hardest to map. Practices operate under different legal entities. Provider counts don't match practice counts. Hospital systems own independent-looking clinics. Without specialized data sources, your market analysis misses half the picture.

Common Questions

Which healthcare specialties can you cover?

All of them. NPI and state licensing data covers every medical specialty. We've done deep analysis in dental, mental health, orthopedics, dermatology, optometry, veterinary, and primary care. If the specialty has a licensing body or NPI taxonomy code, we can map it.

Can you distinguish independent practices from hospital-owned ones?

Yes. Ownership structure is one of the most important targeting dimensions in healthcare and one of the hardest to determine. We use tax ID analysis, CMS affiliation data, and business registration records to identify whether a practice is truly independent, part of a hospital system, or owned by a private equity group.

How current is healthcare provider data?

NPI data is updated monthly. State licensing data varies by state but is generally refreshed quarterly. We cross-reference multiple sources to identify providers who have retired, relocated, or changed affiliations. Healthcare provider data is more volatile than general B2B data because of frequent practice acquisitions and provider turnover.
