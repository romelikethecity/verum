What Churn Analysis Reveals

We analyze your churned accounts alongside your retained ones. The goal is finding what makes the difference. Not general best practices from blog posts. Specific patterns in your data.

Churn by customer segment. Some segments churn at 5% annually. Others at 40%. Knowing which is which changes your acquisition strategy, not just your retention efforts.

Early warning indicators. What behaviors predict churn? For some companies, it's login frequency. For others, it's support ticket sentiment. For others, it's the presence (or absence) of a champion. We find what matters for you.

Time-to-churn patterns. How long do at-risk accounts take to cancel? Is there a window for intervention? When is it too late? Understanding the timeline changes how your team responds.

Churn reason analysis. What do churned customers have in common beyond what they told you? Same industry? Same company size? Same acquisition channel? The real reason is often different from the stated reason.

Revenue impact quantification. Not all churn is equal. We calculate the actual dollar impact by segment so you can prioritize where to focus prevention efforts.

The Process

Step 1: Data collection (1-2 days). Customer records, churn history, usage data if available. We'll specify exactly what we need.

Step 2: Data enrichment (2-3 days). Fill gaps in firmographic data. Add missing company sizes, industries, and other attributes that might correlate with churn.

Step 3: Pattern analysis (5-7 days). Statistical analysis of churned vs retained customers. Segment-level breakdown. Behavioral correlation.

Step 4: Model building (3-4 days). Create predictive indicators based on patterns found. Test against historical data.

Step 5: Delivery and recommendations. Present findings, walk through at-risk accounts, provide prevention strategies.

Total timeline: 2-3 weeks from data handoff to final delivery.

Common Questions

How early can you predict churn?

Typically 60-90 days. Warning signs appear months before cancellation. We find which signals matter for your business specifically.

What data do you need?

Customer records with churn dates, usage data if available, support history, engagement metrics. More behavioral data means better predictions.

Can you analyze churn that already happened?

Yes. Retrospective analysis is actually the best starting point. Understanding past churn informs both prevention and acquisition targeting.

How is this different from a churn dashboard?

Dashboards show churn is happening. Analysis tells you why and what to do. We find patterns and give you prevention strategies.
