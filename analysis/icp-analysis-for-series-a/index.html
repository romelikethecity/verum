Series A Is When ICP Mistakes Get Expensive

At seed stage, you sold to whoever would buy. That was right. You needed customers, revenue, and learning. Now you're Series A. You're about to 3x your sales team. Build out marketing. Target specific segments. If you target wrong, that capital disappears fast.

Your current ICP is probably a guess. Most Series A companies have an ICP document somewhere. It says things like "mid-market SaaS companies" or "B2B marketing teams." That's not specific enough to build a sales motion around. It's not precise enough to tell SDRs who to call.

Early customers aren't random. You have 30, 50, maybe 100 customers. They feel all over the place. But they're not. There are patterns in who bought, who renewed, who expanded, who churned. Those patterns tell you something. You just haven't extracted the signal yet.

Some customers are 5x better than others. Not all revenue is equal. Some customers close fast, expand, renew, and refer. Others grind through a long sales cycle, fight on price, never expand, and churn. Before you scale, you need to know the difference.

The sales motion you build now will persist. Changing ICP after you've hired a team, built playbooks, and trained reps is expensive and slow. Getting it right now is leverage that compounds for years.

What Series A ICP Analysis Involves

We analyze your existing customers to find patterns that predict success. Then we build a precise ICP you can actually execute against.

Customer outcome analysis. Which customers succeeded? How do you define success? Retention, expansion, NPS, support burden. We measure what matters to your business model.

Pattern identification. What characteristics do successful customers share? Company size, industry, tech stack, buying process, use case. We find the combinations that predict outcomes.

Win/loss patterns. Who do you win? Who do you lose? Under what conditions? Win rate isn't one number. It's a matrix by segment and situation.

Churn risk factors. Which customer types churn? When? Why? Understanding churn patterns is as important as understanding wins.

Expansion potential. Which customers grow with you? What triggers expansion? These customers are worth more and should be weighted in ICP.

Step 4: ICP synthesis (2-3 days). Build the ICP profile, scoring framework, and prioritization matrix.
