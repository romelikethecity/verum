Fintech Market Analysis

Fintech market analysis provides a data-driven view of the financial technology landscape: how many companies exist by sub-sector, what technologies they use, where funding is concentrated, and where competitive density varies. We build this from company-level data for actionable market intelligence.

The fintech market is projected at $500+ billion. But that number includes everything from JPMorgan's digital banking to a two-person crypto startup. You need to know how many companies match your specific fintech ICP, what their tech stacks look like, and who else is selling to them.

Fintech Market Analysis Process

  • Sub-sector mapping. We segment the fintech landscape by sub-sector: payments, lending, insurance tech, wealth management, banking infrastructure, crypto/blockchain, regtech, and others.
  • Company enumeration. We count companies in each sub-sector by size, funding stage, geography, and business model (B2B vs B2C, platform vs point solution).
  • Funding analysis. We map funding patterns to show which sub-sectors are attracting capital, which are consolidating, and where growth is concentrated.
  • Technology landscape. We identify common technology stacks in fintech to show which tools are adopted widely and where integration or replacement opportunities exist.

Fintech Market Intelligence

  • Company counts by fintech sub-sector with firmographic profiles for targeting
  • Funding flow analysis showing which segments are growing and which are contracting
  • Technology adoption data revealing which tools fintech companies use and where market gaps exist
  • Competitive density mapping showing where incumbents are strongest and where new entrants have opportunity

Common Questions

How do you define fintech companies?

We use a combination of industry classification, product description, and technology signals. A company is fintech if its primary product or service applies technology to financial services. We include both pure-play fintechs and the technology divisions of traditional financial institutions. You can customize the definition for your analysis.

Can you analyze a specific fintech sub-sector in depth?

Yes. If you're focused specifically on payments infrastructure or lending platforms or insurance tech, we can go deeper into that sub-sector with more granular company profiling, technology analysis, and competitive mapping than a broader fintech overview would provide.

How fast does fintech market data change?

Fast. Fintech has higher startup formation and failure rates than most industries, and frequent M&A activity. Funding data changes quarterly. Company counts shift noticeably every 6 months. We recommend refreshing fintech market analysis every 6 months for active go-to-market planning.
