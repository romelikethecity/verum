You're Pursuing Deals You Can't Win

Every quarter, your sales team spends cycles on deals that were never going to close. The prospect seemed like a fit. They took meetings. They asked for a proposal. Then they went dark, chose a competitor, or decided to do nothing. Your reps are back to square one.

The pattern repeats because nobody's looking at the data systematically.

Lost deal reasons are guesses, not facts. "Went with competitor." "Budget constraints." "Timing wasn't right." Reps log what the prospect said, which is rarely the real reason. And nobody aggregates these to find patterns.

Win rate varies wildly by segment, but you don't know which. Some company types close at 60%. Others close at 7%. Without analysis, you treat them the same. Your SDRs fill the pipeline with low-probability deals that clog the forecast.

Competitive dynamics are black boxes. Which competitors do you beat? Which beat you? Under what conditions? Your reps have opinions. Data has answers.

Deal velocity patterns go unnoticed. Some deals close in 30 days. Others drag for 6 months and still lose. Fast-moving deals have characteristics. Knowing what predicts velocity would change qualification.

A 20% win rate isn't a ceiling. It's an average that masks huge variation. Some segments might be 50%. Others might be 5%. Finding the difference changes everything.

What Win-Loss Analysis Reveals

We analyze your closed deals (won and lost) to find what actually drives outcomes. Not opinions. Patterns in the data.

Win rate by segment. Which company sizes, industries, and use cases do you close at high rates? Which have dismal conversion? This becomes your prioritization framework.

Competitive win rates. When you face Competitor A, what happens? What about Competitor B? Under what conditions do you win competitive deals? When should you walk away?

Deal velocity patterns. What predicts fast closes? Slow closes? Deals that stall? Velocity correlates with certain prospect characteristics and deal structures.

Deal size correlation. Do larger deals take longer? Have lower win rates? Require different sales motion? Understanding the tradeoffs helps you decide which deals to pursue.

Stage conversion analysis. Where do deals die in your pipeline? Are there stages where certain segments get stuck? Knowing this helps both forecasting and qualification.

The Process

Step 1: Data collection (1-2 days). CRM export of closed deals. Won and lost. As much history as you have.

Step 2: Data enrichment (2-3 days). Fill firmographic gaps. Your CRM probably has incomplete data on lost deals. We fix that.

Step 3: Pattern analysis (5-7 days). Statistical analysis of win rates across dimensions. Segment the data multiple ways to find meaningful patterns.

Step 4: Synthesis (2-3 days). Turn patterns into actionable guidance. Qualification criteria. Competitive positioning. Forecasting adjustments.

Step 5: Delivery and review. Present findings. Answer questions. Help you figure out how to operationalize insights.

Common Questions

How many deals do I need?

At least 50 closed deals combined. Ideally 100+. More deals mean more confident conclusions. We can work with less but findings will be more directional.

Do you interview lost prospects?

That's a separate service. Our core analysis uses CRM data and enrichment. Interview-based research adds depth but takes longer and costs more.

What if our lost deal data is incomplete?

Most is. Reps log won deals carefully, lost deals loosely. We supplement with enrichment. Even imperfect data reveals valuable patterns.

How is this different from pipeline reports?

Reports show conversion rates. Analysis explains why. Which prospects convert? What predicts outcomes? Analysis goes deeper than metrics.
