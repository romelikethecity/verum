The Construction Data Problem

You sell to contractors, developers, or construction companies. Your database has thousands of records. But when your sales team actually tries to use it, the problems start.

Phone numbers that ring disconnected. Emails that bounce. Contacts who left the company two years ago. Companies that went out of business. Your reps waste hours chasing dead leads while real opportunities sit buried in the noise.

Construction industry data is uniquely challenging. And most companies just accept the mess.

Construction companies change constantly

Contractors form new companies, dissolve old ones, merge with partners. The GC you quoted last year might be operating under a different name now. Your database doesn't know. Your reps keep calling the old number, wondering why nobody picks up.

Contact information goes stale fast

Project managers move between firms. Estimators get promoted. Owners retire. The construction industry has high turnover at every level. That contact list you bought six months ago? A quarter of it is already outdated.

Phone numbers are particularly unreliable

Construction runs on phone calls, not emails. But your phone data is the worst. Cell numbers change. Office lines get disconnected. Wrong numbers pile up. Your reps dial ten numbers to reach one person.

Duplicates multiply across sources

Same contractor in your database five times. Once from a trade show. Once from permit data. Once from a project bid. Once from a referral. Once from a purchased list. Different phone numbers, different spellings, different contact names. Nobody knows which record is right.

Bad data doesn't just slow down your sales team. It costs you deals. While your reps chase dead leads, your competitors with clean data are already talking to decision-makers.

Common Questions About Construction Data Cleaning

How long does data cleaning take?

Most projects complete in 24-48 hours. Larger datasets might take a few days. We'll give you a realistic timeline before we start.

What types of construction data do you clean?

Contractor databases, developer lists, subcontractor contacts, supplier records, permit data extracts, and any B2B data you use to reach the construction industry.

Can you validate contractor license information?

We validate company names against business registries and can flag records for license verification. For active license status checking, we recommend pairing our cleaning with state licensing board lookups, which vary by state.

What file formats do you work with?

CSV, Excel, database exports. If your CRM or system can export it, we can clean it. We return data in whatever format works for your import.

How do you handle the same company with multiple locations?

We can either merge to a single record or keep locations separate with standardized parent company information. We'll discuss your preference during the discovery call.

Can you clean data from permit databases?

Yes. Permit data is notoriously messy with inconsistent company names and outdated contact info. We can standardize and validate it like any other data source.
