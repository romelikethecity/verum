The Construction Data Quality Problem

You're a general contractor, construction supplier, or service provider to the industry. Your CRM and project management systems hold years of contractor, subcontractor, and project contact data. But that data has become unreliable.

Construction has unique data challenges. Subcontractors work under different DBAs. Estimators and project managers move between companies constantly. License and insurance information expires. And every project brings in new contacts that get added without checking for duplicates. Your team wastes time verifying information instead of bidding and building.

Subcontractors are duplicated everywhere

"ABC Electrical" and "ABC Electric LLC" and "ABC Electrical Services" are all in your database. Maybe they're the same company. Maybe they're different. When you're putting together a bid, you can't easily see that contractor's full project history because it's scattered across duplicate records.

Contacts are constantly moving

The estimator who worked on your last project is now at a competitor. The project manager retired. Construction has high mobility, and every job change creates stale contact data. You're reaching out to people who haven't been at that company in years.

License and insurance data is stale

Contractor licenses expire. Insurance certificates lapse. Your approved vendor list shows credentials that were current two years ago. Compliance requirements mean you need current data, but manual verification is time-consuming.

Trade classifications are inconsistent

Is this contractor "electrical" or "low voltage" or "fire alarm"? Different projects categorized them differently. Your trade directories are a mess of inconsistent classifications that make it hard to find qualified subs when you need them.
