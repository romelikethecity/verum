The Media Lead Data Problem

You're selling to media and entertainment companies. Publishers, streamers, studios, gaming companies. Your buyers are busy executives who get pitched constantly. Generic outreach doesn't work. You need precise targeting and relevant positioning.

Media buyer data has unique challenges. Executive contact information is protected. Audience and reach metrics help qualify but are scattered across sources. The line between editorial and business decisions varies by company. And media is in constant flux, with properties launching, merging, and shutting down constantly.

Business-side contacts are hard to reach

You want to reach the CRO, CPO, or Head of Ad Ops. Not the editorial contacts that are published everywhere. Business decision-makers at media companies protect their time and contact information.

Audience data helps qualification

A publisher with 50M monthly visitors has different needs than one with 500K. Audience size and composition affect deal size and fit. But this data is scattered across analytics platforms and press releases.

Property hierarchies matter

Are you selling to the individual publication or the parent company? Where do decisions get made? You need to understand conglomerate structures to know who to approach and at what level.

Tech stack indicates fit

What CMS do they use? What ad server? What video platform? If you're selling media technology, knowing their current stack is essential for positioning. But media companies don't advertise their tech infrastructure.
