The Media Data Problem

It's upfront season. Your ad sales team is reaching out to agencies and brands with next year's inventory. Relationships you've built over years. Premium placements. Strong packages.

Except the media buyer you've been working with moved to a different agency. The brand contact who approved last year's buy switched companies. The agency that handled your biggest account lost the business and you didn't know. Your outreach is hitting inboxes of people who can't buy anymore.

The ad industry moves fast. Your data isn't keeping up.

Agency turnover reshuffles your contacts constantly

Agencies have notoriously high turnover. Media buyers, planners, and account leads jump between agencies regularly. The buyer you knew at one agency might be at a competitor next quarter. Or in-house at a brand. Or out of the industry entirely. Your CRM reflects relationships that may no longer exist.

Brand-agency relationships shift without notice

Accounts get reviewed. Agencies win and lose business. The agency handling your biggest advertiser's media might not have that account anymore. You're pitching to the wrong people because nobody told you the relationship changed.

Brand-side marketing teams restructure often

Budget cuts, reorgs, in-housing, outsourcing. The brand-side marketing leader who approved direct buys might be gone. Their replacement might want to run everything through an agency. Your direct relationships need constant updating.

Contact data fragments across properties

If you're selling across multiple media properties, you might have different contact data in each system. The same agency relationship shows up differently in digital, broadcast, and print databases. No single view of the relationship.

Every email to someone who left is an opportunity missed. Every pitch to an agency that lost the account is time wasted. Every relationship you don't know about is revenue going to competitors. In a market measured in billions, clean data isn't optional.
