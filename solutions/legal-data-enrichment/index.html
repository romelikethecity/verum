The Legal Data Gap Problem

You're selling to law firms. You know your target firms and practice areas. But your CRM has firm names and maybe a general phone number. That's not enough to run an effective sales motion.

Law firm data is notoriously hard to get. Firms protect their attorneys' contact information. Decision-makers for technology purchases often aren't listed on websites. Practice area specializations aren't standardized. Your SDRs are spending hours researching each firm just to find the right person to contact.

Contact data is protected

Law firm websites list attorney names but rarely direct contact information. Main office numbers go to receptionists. Generic emails go to marketing departments. Without verified direct contact data, you're not reaching decision-makers.

Decision-makers aren't always attorneys

If you're selling technology, the buyers might be the CIO, legal ops director, or innovation partner. These roles aren't always easy to find, and they're rarely the people featured on firm websites.

Practice area data is incomplete

You want to target M&A partners or patent prosecutors. But firm websites often list attorneys under broad categories like "Litigation" without the granularity you need for targeting. You need verified practice area data to segment effectively.

Firm-level data is scattered

Headcount, revenue, profits per partner, office locations, tech stack. The firmographic data that determines fit is spread across rankings, press releases, and proprietary databases. Nobody has time to compile it manually.
