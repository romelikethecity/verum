The Education Data Quality Problem

You're selling to schools, districts, or universities. Your CRM holds years of institution records and administrator contacts. But that data has become unreliable.

Education data has unique challenges. Administrators turn over frequently, especially principals. Schools change names, merge, or close. District structures reorganize. And your sales team has added records from multiple sources over years, creating duplicates and inconsistencies.

Institutions are duplicated

"Lincoln Elementary" and "Lincoln Elementary School" and "Lincoln ES" are all in your database. Maybe they're the same school. Maybe they're different schools in different districts. When you can't trust institution data, your territory planning and account coverage suffer.

Administrator turnover is high

Principals and department heads change roles frequently. The superintendent you pitched last year retired. The curriculum director moved to another district. Education has high administrator turnover, and every change creates stale contact data.

District affiliations are outdated

Districts merge, split, and reorganize. Charter networks acquire schools. Your database shows institutional affiliations that were accurate years ago but don't reflect current reality. District-level sales motions fail when the data is wrong.

Contact information is inconsistent

Some contacts have school emails, some have personal, some have both. Phone extensions change. Buildings move. Your outreach fails when you're reaching out to contacts with outdated information.
