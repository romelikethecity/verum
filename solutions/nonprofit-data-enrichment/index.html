The Nonprofit Prospect Data Problem

You're running development operations for a nonprofit. Your CRM has donor names and giving history. But you're missing the data you need to identify major gift prospects and cultivate them effectively.

Prospect research is time-consuming. Your development team spends hours researching individual donors instead of building relationships. Meanwhile, hidden major gift prospects sit in your database unidentified because you lack the capacity indicators and wealth data to find them.

Capacity is hidden

Who among your donors has major gift potential? The data that indicates wealth, real estate ownership, business interests, and stock holdings isn't in your CRM. Your major gift officers don't know where to focus.

Employment data is outdated

Your donor records show employers from when they first gave. People change jobs, get promoted, and retire. Without current employment data, you can't identify matching gift eligibility or leverage corporate connections.

Philanthropic history is incomplete

Where else do your donors give? What causes do they support? Foundation board memberships? Philanthropic history indicates giving capacity and affinity, but you don't have this data across your donor base.

Foundation data is fragmentary

For institutional fundraising, you need foundation giving history, focus areas, and current contacts. But this data is scattered across multiple sources and hard to aggregate.
