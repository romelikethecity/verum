The Logistics Lead Data Problem

You're selling logistics services, freight technology, or building carrier capacity. Your CRM has company names and maybe a main number. That's not enough to win shipper accounts or recruit quality carriers.

Logistics contact data is challenging. Shippers protect their logistics teams from cold calls. Carrier contacts change frequently. Facility locations matter for lane development. Your sales team spends hours researching each prospect before they can start outreach.

Logistics contacts are hidden

Traffic managers and supply chain directors don't list direct phone numbers. Carrier dispatchers use personal cells that aren't public. Without verified contact data, you're not reaching the decision-makers who control freight.

Freight volume is unclear

Which companies ship significant volume? You can't always tell from basic firmographics. A manufacturer with multiple DCs has different needs than a single-location operation. You need signals about freight intensity.

Facility locations are incomplete

For lane development, you need to know where shippers have facilities. Origin and destination data drives your capacity planning. But facility-level data isn't in your CRM.

Carrier capacity is hard to assess

For brokerages and shippers building carrier networks, fleet size, equipment types, and lane experience matter. But this data requires research across multiple sources.
