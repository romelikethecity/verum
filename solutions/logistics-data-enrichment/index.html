The Logistics Data Problem

It's peak season. You need capacity for a major customer. Your TMS shows 50 carriers who've hauled this lane before. Good rates. Proven performance. Should be straightforward.

First dispatcher's number goes straight to voicemail. Message never returned. Second carrier's MC number shows revoked authority. Third one went out of business three months ago. Fourth one's dispatcher quit and nobody updated your system. You're scrambling to find capacity while your customer waits.

Freight doesn't wait for your database to catch up.

Driver turnover ripples through everything

The trucking industry has turnover rates above 90%. When drivers leave, dispatch staff often follow. That dispatcher you built a relationship with? There's a good chance they're at a different carrier now. Or out of the industry entirely. Your contact data ages faster in logistics than almost any other sector.

Small carriers appear and disappear constantly

The trucking industry has over 900,000 carriers. Most are small. Many are fragile. Thousands of trucking companies fail or cease operations every year. Others get acquired or merge. Your carrier database is accumulating dead entries faster than you realize.

Insurance and authority status changes without notice

A carrier's insurance lapses. Their authority gets revoked. FMCSA status changes. None of this automatically updates in your TMS. You find out when you're trying to tender a load and discover you can't use a carrier you were counting on.

Shipper contact decay hurts sales too

It's not just carriers. If you're selling freight services, your shipper contacts decay just as fast. Transportation managers change roles. Companies restructure their supply chains. The logistics buyer you knew might be gone before your next renewal conversation.

Every disconnected number is a load that almost didn't move. Every dead carrier is capacity you can't use. Every outdated shipper contact is revenue walking out the door. Your competitors with clean data are covering loads while you're leaving messages that won't get returned.
