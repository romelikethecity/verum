The HR Tech Sales Data Problem

You sell recruiting software, HRIS, benefits platforms, or people analytics tools. Your CRM is full of HR leaders across thousands of companies. But HR leadership changes constantly.

That VP of People you've been nurturing? Left to join a startup. The CHRO who showed interest? Retired last quarter. The Head of Talent Acquisition on your target list? Position was eliminated in restructuring. Your database reflects an HR landscape that no longer exists.

HR tech sales data has unique challenges. High turnover, constantly evolving titles, and the way companies restructure people functions mean your prospect data goes stale fast.

HR leaders change jobs frequently

CHRO tenure averages around 3.5 years—shorter than most C-suite roles. VPs of People, Heads of HR, and talent leaders move even more often. That contact list from last year? A significant portion has already moved on.

HR titles are inconsistent

"Chief People Officer," "VP People," "CHRO," "Head of Human Resources," "Chief Human Resources Officer"—all potentially the same role at the same level. Your segmentation breaks when the same buyer persona appears under a dozen different titles.

Company restructuring affects HR first

When companies grow, shrink, or reorganize, HR structure changes. Roles get consolidated. Departments get split. The "VP of HR" becomes "VP of People & Culture" or gets absorbed into a "Chief Administrative Officer" role. Your data doesn't keep up.

Purchased lists compound the problem

You've bought HR leader databases, CHRO lists, people ops contact data. Each list has different title conventions, different ages, different accuracy levels. Now you have the same person five times with different titles and companies.

Clean data is your competitive advantage in HR tech. While others send emails to people who left months ago, you could be building relationships with actual decision-makers.
