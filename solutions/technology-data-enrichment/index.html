The Technology Data Problem

It's pipeline review. Your SDR team is working a list of 500 tech companies that downloaded your whitepaper six months ago. Good intent signal. Should be quality meetings.

Except company #1 ran out of runway and shut down. Company #2 pivoted from B2B to B2C. Company #3 got acquired and the entire engineering team was let go. Company #4's VP of Engineering, the person who downloaded your content, is now at a different company entirely. Your "hot leads" are pointing at ghosts.

Tech moves faster than any other industry. Your database is already out of date.

Startup mortality creates constant churn

Most startups fail. The ones that survive often pivot dramatically, sometimes multiple times. That Series A company you identified as a prospect might not exist in any recognizable form a year later. Or at all. Your prospect list is accumulating dead companies faster than you realize.

M&A activity reshapes your market constantly

Tech acquisitions happen constantly. When companies get acquired, contacts scatter. Some stay with the acquirer. Some leave. Some end up at the acquiring company but in completely different roles. Your carefully built contact list fragments with every deal announcement you missed.

Tech layoffs come in waves

The tech industry experiences hiring booms and layoff waves. When a company cuts 20% of their workforce, there's a good chance your contact was in that 20%. Especially engineering leaders, product managers, and technical decision-makers. Your champion might be job hunting, not buying software.

Tech stack data goes stale quickly

Companies change their technology constantly. That company using a competitor's tool last year might have switched. That startup you passed on because they weren't using your ecosystem might have migrated. Without current technographic data, you're making targeting decisions on outdated information.

Every email to a dead company is time wasted. Every pitch to someone who got laid off is a meeting that won't happen. Every prospect whose tech stack you don't know is a conversation that might miss the mark. In a market moving this fast, yesterday's data is already wrong.
