The Technology Lead Data Problem

You're selling to tech companies. Startups, scale-ups, enterprises. Your CRM has company names and maybe some product signup data. That's not enough to run an effective sales motion in the crowded tech market.

Tech buyer data has unique challenges. Engineers are notoriously hard to reach through cold outreach. Decision-making is distributed across technical and business roles. And the signals that indicate buying readiness, like funding rounds and hiring, change quickly. Your SDRs spend hours researching each account before they can start prospecting.

Technical buyers are hard to reach

Engineers and technical leaders don't respond to generic sales emails. They block cold calls. Their emails are protected. Without verified direct contact information and the context to personalize your approach, you're not breaking through.

Buying committees are complex

A developer tools purchase might involve the VP of Engineering, a platform team lead, DevOps, and procurement. You need the full buying committee, not just a single contact. And you need to understand who evaluates versus who approves budget.

Firmographic data changes fast

Tech companies grow, contract, pivot, and get acquired at faster rates than any other industry. The headcount and funding data from six months ago is probably wrong. You need current data to qualify effectively.

Tech stack matters for positioning

If you're selling infrastructure or developer tools, knowing what they're currently using is essential. You need to know if they're an AWS or GCP shop, what languages they use, what monitoring tools they have. But companies don't advertise this.
