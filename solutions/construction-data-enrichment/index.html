The Construction Data Problem

It's bid day. You need to pull together quotes from your trusted electrical subs for a commercial project downtown. You've worked with three of them before. Good relationships. Reliable work.

First one's number is disconnected. Second one's email bounces. Third one closed shop during the pandemic. Your "trusted subcontractor list" is suddenly a lot shorter than you thought. The bid is due tomorrow.

You start cold-calling electrical contractors you've never worked with, hoping to find someone with capacity and credentials before the deadline.

The construction industry churns faster than your database

About one in five construction businesses fail within their first five years. Others get acquired, change names, or quietly stop taking new projects. The subcontractor who did great work on your last job might not be answering their phone today. Your vendor database doesn't update itself.

Project managers and estimators move constantly

Contact data decays at roughly 30% per year across all industries. In construction, with its project-based nature and job-to-job movement, turnover can be even higher. The estimator you knew at ABC Mechanical? She's at XYZ now. Your CRM still shows her old email.

Qualification and licensing data goes stale

Certifications expire. Insurance lapses. Licenses need renewal. The fact that a sub was qualified last year doesn't mean they're qualified today. But tracking all of that manually across hundreds of subcontractors is a full-time job nobody has time for.

Regional expansion multiplies the problem

When you take on projects in new markets, you need to build subcontractor networks fast. But finding reliable subs in an unfamiliar area means starting from scratch. Your existing relationships don't translate, and you don't have time to vet everyone properly before the first bid.

Every dead phone number is a bid that almost didn't happen. Every email bounce is a relationship that drifted away. Every expired license you missed is a compliance risk you didn't see coming.

Common Questions About Construction Data Enrichment

How long does implementation take?

There isn't really an "implementation." Send us a file, we send back clean data. First project typically completes in 24-48 hours. No IT project required.

Can you verify contractor licenses?

We can flag license data for verification and cross-reference against available public databases. For official verification, we recommend checking with your state licensing board, but we can identify which records need attention and which companies appear to have current credentials.

Do you work with construction management software?

Yes. We've worked with exports from Procore, Buildertrend, CoConstruct, and most major platforms. If your system can export data, we can clean it. We'll return it in whatever format you need.

How is this different from buying a ZoomInfo license?

Three fundamental differences:

Different problem. ZoomInfo sells access to their contact database. If you need net-new subcontractor names, that's their strength. But if you already have years of subcontractor relationships and now that data is stale? That's us. We clean what you have.

Different pricing. ZoomInfo charges recurring annual licenses, typically $15K-$50K+ per year. We charge per project. Clean your data once, pay once.

Different ownership. ZoomInfo's terms require you to delete their data if you cancel. With Verum, your cleaned data is yours forever.

What about data security?

Encrypted transfers, no data sharing with third parties, deletion after project completion unless you request otherwise. Your subcontractor network is competitive intelligence. We treat it that way.

Do you work with companies of our size?

We work with local contractors and national GCs. Data quality challenges are similar regardless of project volume. A sub list with 200 contacts has the same decay issues as one with 20,000.
