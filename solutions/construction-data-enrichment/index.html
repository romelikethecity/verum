The Construction Lead Data Problem

You're selling to contractors, builders, or construction owner organizations. Your CRM has company names and maybe a general office number. That's not enough to run an effective sales motion in construction.

Construction data is notoriously hard to get. Contractors don't put their project managers' direct lines online. Decision-makers vary by project type and size. Firmographic data like annual revenue and specialty focus lives in scattered databases. Your SDRs spend hours researching each account before they can start prospecting.

Contacts are hidden

General contractors protect their estimators and project managers. You might find a main office number, but good luck reaching the PM who actually specifies materials and equipment. Generic emails go to reception. Without direct contact data, you're not getting through.

Company size is unclear

Is this a $10M regional contractor or a $500M national GC? Annual revenue determines everything about how you sell to them. But construction companies don't publish their financials, and employee count is a rough proxy at best.

Specialty focus is hard to determine

Does this contractor do commercial, residential, industrial, or all three? Healthcare projects require different qualifications than warehouse builds. Understanding their specialty focus is essential for qualification, but it's not always obvious from a company name.

Project activity is invisible

Who has an active backlog? Who's bidding new work? Timing matters in construction sales. If you catch a contractor during bidding, your product might make it into the spec. After award, you're competing against relationships already established.
