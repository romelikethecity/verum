The AdTech Data Problem

It's campaign launch day. Your programmatic team has built segments based on your customer data. Job titles, company sizes, industries. The targeting looks precise on paper.

Two weeks later, the results come in. Click-through rates are half what you projected. Conversion rates are worse. Your data partner shrugs. Your client asks hard questions. Someone pulls a sample and discovers that 30% of the "CMOs" in your segment changed jobs in the last year. The titles are wrong. The companies are wrong. The whole foundation was rotten.

This happens more than anyone in adtech wants to admit.

Audience data decays faster than campaigns refresh

B2B contact data degrades at roughly 30% per year. Job changes, company changes, retirements, promotions. Your carefully built segments from six months ago are already significantly stale. The CMO you're targeting might be a VP somewhere else now. The company that fit your ICP might have been acquired.

Third-party data is getting harder to trust

Cookie deprecation isn't just a Chrome story anymore. Privacy regulations are tightening everywhere. The third-party data providers you relied on for years are scrambling to adapt. Their data quality is suffering. Meanwhile, your campaigns still need to hit numbers.

First-party data is messy

Everyone says first-party data is the answer. And it is. But most adtech companies have years of accumulated customer and prospect data that's never been properly cleaned. Duplicates everywhere. Outdated contacts. Companies that merged or went out of business. You can't build good segments on a bad foundation.

Identity resolution is only as good as your inputs

All those fancy identity graphs and cross-device matching tools? They amplify whatever data quality issues you already have. Bad data in, confidently wrong outputs out. The algorithms don't know your contact list is 30% stale. They just keep matching against bad records.

Every impression served to the wrong person is money wasted. Every campaign built on decayed segments is a client relationship at risk. The adtech companies winning right now are the ones with clean data foundations.
