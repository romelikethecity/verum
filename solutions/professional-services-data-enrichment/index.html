The Professional Services Lead Data Problem

You're selling high-value consulting and advisory services. Strategy, operations, technology transformation, financial advisory. Your clients are senior executives who don't respond to generic outreach. You need precise targeting and personalized approaches.

But executive data is hard to get. C-suite contacts protect their time. Direct lines aren't published. LinkedIn shows titles but not contact info. And by the time you've researched a target thoroughly, the window for relevant outreach may have closed.

Executive contacts are protected

CFOs, CIOs, and CHROs don't put their direct numbers online. General company lines go through gatekeepers. Generic emails get filtered. Without verified direct contact information, your partners are cold calling reception desks.

Timing matters enormously

Consulting engagements often follow trigger events: new executives, M&A announcements, funding rounds, regulatory changes. By the time these are public knowledge, competitors may already be in conversations. You need to know about triggers as they happen.

Company context is fragmented

What initiatives is the company pursuing? What's their current strategic focus? Who are the key stakeholders? This context lives across news articles, press releases, and industry publications. Nobody has time to compile it manually for every target.

Decision-makers are distributed

A transformation engagement might involve the CEO, CFO, CIO, and COO. You need the full buying committee, not just a single contact. And you need to understand reporting relationships and influence dynamics.
