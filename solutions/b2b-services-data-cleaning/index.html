The B2B Services Data Quality Problem

You're running an agency, consultancy, or professional services firm. Your CRM holds years of client relationships, project history, and prospect data. But that data has become fragmented and unreliable.

B2B services have unique data challenges. Clients engage multiple service lines. Contacts move between companies frequently. Projects get entered by different teams with inconsistent naming. And your business development team keeps adding prospects without checking for existing relationships. Your partners can't see the full client picture when they need it most.

Clients are fragmented across service lines

Your marketing team worked with Acme Corp. Your consulting team has a separate Acme record. Your analytics group created a third. Now you have three incomplete views of what should be a single strategic client relationship. Cross-sell opportunities get missed. Relationship history is scattered.

Contact information is outdated

The CMO you worked with two years ago is now at a different company. The procurement contact retired. B2B services relationships depend on people, and people move. Your outreach efforts fail when you're reaching out to contacts who left.

Prospect data is cluttered

Three years of conference leads, webinar signups, and BD outreach have accumulated. Most of those prospects have moved on, changed roles, or aren't relevant anymore. Your pipeline shows thousands of "opportunities" that will never close.

Company records are inconsistent

"Acme" and "Acme Inc" and "Acme Corporation" are all in your database. Subsidiaries are mixed with parent companies. Acquired companies still show under old names. Your account-based reporting doesn't work when the same company appears multiple ways.
