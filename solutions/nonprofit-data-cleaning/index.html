The Nonprofit Data Quality Problem

You're running development and fundraising operations for a nonprofit. Your CRM holds years of donor relationships, giving history, and constituent data. But that data has become fragmented and unreliable.

Nonprofit data has unique challenges. Donors move frequently. Name variations create duplicates. Household relationships break when spouses have different last names. And decades of data entry by volunteers and staff have created inconsistencies throughout your database.

Donors are duplicated everywhere

"Robert Smith" and "Bob Smith" and "R. Smith" are all in your database. Maybe they're the same major donor. Maybe they're different people. Your giving reports show inflated donor counts. Your stewardship is fractured because you can't see the full relationship.

Contact information is outdated

Donors move. Email addresses change. Phone numbers update. Your annual fund appeals return to sender. Your cultivation emails bounce. You're spending money on communications that never reach your constituents.

Household relationships are broken

Married couples should be linked. But when spouses have different last names or were entered separately, they appear as unrelated donors. Your major gift officers can't see household giving capacity. Your recognition levels are wrong.

Corporate and foundation data is inconsistent

The same foundation appears under different names. Corporate sponsor contacts have changed. Grant maker data from different sources doesn't match. Your institutional fundraising is hampered by messy organizational records.
