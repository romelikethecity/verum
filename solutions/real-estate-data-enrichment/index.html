The Real Estate Data Gap Problem

You're selling to real estate professionals. You know your target markets and agent profiles. But your CRM has names and maybe office phone numbers. That's not enough to reach busy agents who are constantly on the go.

Real estate is a massive, fragmented industry. 1.5 million active agents across thousands of brokerages. Most agents work as independent contractors with minimal online presence. Your SDRs are spending hours researching each prospect just to find contact information that works.

Contact data is hard to find

Agents don't have corporate email addresses. Office phone numbers go to brokerage front desks. Personal cell phones are guarded carefully. Without verified direct contact data, you're playing phone tag with receptionists instead of talking to agents.

Production data is scattered

You want to target top producers, not part-time agents. But transaction volume data is locked in MLS systems, scattered across public records, and rarely attached to contact records. You can't tell the $10M producer from the $100K one.

Brokerage affiliation changes constantly

The agent's profile says RE/MAX but they moved to Compass last month. Without current brokerage data, your outreach references the wrong company and gets ignored.

License and designation data is missing

Is this agent actually licensed? Do they have their broker's license? Any designations like CRS or ABR? These qualifications matter for targeting but they're rarely in CRM records.
