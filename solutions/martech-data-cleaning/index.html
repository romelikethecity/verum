The Martech Sales Data Problem

You sell marketing automation, analytics, content tools, or advertising technology. Your CRM is full of marketers across thousands of companies. But marketing leadership changes constantly.

That CMO you've been nurturing? Left for another company six months ago. The VP of Marketing who showed interest? Her startup got acquired and role was eliminated. The Head of Demand Gen on your target list? Promoted and now has a different title with different priorities. Your database reflects a marketing landscape that no longer exists.

Martech sales data has unique challenges. Short CMO tenures, constantly evolving marketing titles, and the way companies restructure marketing functions mean your prospect data goes stale fast.

Marketing leaders change jobs constantly

CMO tenure averages around 2.5 years—the shortest in the C-suite. VPs of Marketing, Heads of Growth, and demand gen leaders move even more frequently. That contact list from last year? A significant portion has already moved on.

Marketing titles are wildly inconsistent

"CMO," "Chief Growth Officer," "VP Marketing," "Head of Growth," "VP Demand Generation"—all potentially similar roles with different responsibilities at different companies. Your segmentation breaks when the same buyer persona appears under dozens of different titles.

Marketing teams restructure constantly

Growth teams spin up and dissolve. Demand gen merges with sales ops. Content marketing gets absorbed into product marketing. The "VP of Digital Marketing" becomes "VP of Growth" or reports to a newly created "Chief Revenue Officer." Your data doesn't keep up.

Purchased lists compound the problem

You've bought marketing leader databases, CMO lists, demand gen contact data. Each list has different title conventions, different ages, different accuracy levels. Now you have the same person five times with different titles and companies.

Clean data is your competitive advantage in martech. While others send emails to people who left months ago, you could be building relationships with actual decision-makers.
