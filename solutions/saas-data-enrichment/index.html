The SaaS Sales Data Problem

It's 2pm on a Tuesday. Your SDR has a list of 50 accounts to work through. Good accounts. Companies that match your ICP perfectly on paper.

First five calls: two disconnected numbers, one person who left last quarter, one company that was acquired and doesn't need your solution anymore, and one valid prospect who's actually interested. Twenty percent success rate, and that's considered pretty good.

By the end of the day, your rep has made 40 dials and had 6 real conversations. The other 34 attempts? Wasted time. Bad data.

This plays out across SaaS sales floors everywhere. The data decays faster than anyone can fix it manually, and your team pays the price.

Your contact data is rotting faster than you think

According to Landbase's 2024 analysis, B2B contact data decays between 22.5% and 70.3% annually. In SaaS specifically? The numbers skew toward the high end. People change jobs constantly in tech. That champion you sold to last year has probably moved twice since then.

Reps spend more time researching than selling

Salesforce's State of Sales report found that reps spend only 30% of their time actively selling. The rest goes to writing emails, entering data, researching leads, and sitting in internal meetings. And 71% of sales reps say they spend too much time on data entry. That's not a process problem. That's a data problem.

Bad data is actively hurting your revenue

IBM research found that incorrect data cuts into 27% of potential revenue. Not 2.7%. Twenty-seven percent. Your reps are wasting 546 hours annually pursuing bad leads. That's over 13 weeks of selling time, gone.

The performance paradox is real

Here's what's strange: 79% of sales teams increased revenue last year, but 70% of individual reps missed quota. How? Fewer reps are carrying more of the load. The top performers are crushing it while everyone else struggles with efficiency problems that clean data would solve.

Every bad phone number is a wasted dial. Every outdated email is a bounced campaign. Every contact who left is a conversation that goes nowhere. Your team deserves better tools than a CRM full of decay.
