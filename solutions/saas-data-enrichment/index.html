The SaaS Lead Data Problem

Your marketing machine is generating leads. Content downloads, webinar signups, demo requests, free trial conversions. The volume is there. But the data quality? That's where things break down.

A lead comes in with a name and email. Maybe a company name, probably misspelled. Maybe a phone number, probably the main line. Now your SDR has to spend 15 minutes researching before they can even decide if this lead is worth calling. Multiply that by 50 leads a day and you've got a productivity problem.

Form fills are incomplete

Nobody wants to fill out 15 fields on a form. So you keep forms short, which means leads come in with minimal data. Name, email, company. That's not enough to prioritize, route, or personalize. Your SDRs are manually researching every lead to fill in the gaps.

Tech stack data is invisible

You sell to companies using Salesforce, or companies not using any CRM, or companies using your competitor. But you can't tell which is which from a form fill. Without tech stack data, you're flying blind on fit. Your best leads look identical to your worst ones.

Contact data is shallow

You have one contact at each company. Maybe. But B2B sales requires multi-threading. You need the VP of Sales and the CTO and the CFO. Building out the buying committee manually takes hours per account.

Firmographic data is missing

Company size, revenue, industry, funding stage. The data points that determine fit. When forms don't capture them and CRMs don't have them, your lead scoring is guesswork and your routing is broken.
