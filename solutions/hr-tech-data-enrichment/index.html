The HR Tech Sales Data Problem

It's the start of Q1. Budget season. Your sales team has a list of 200 HR leaders who expressed interest last year but didn't close. Perfect timing to re-engage.

First thirty calls: five people who left the company entirely, four who moved from HR into operations, three disconnected numbers, eight voicemails to people no longer in HR, and ten actual conversations. Two of those ten say they just signed with a competitor last month.

In a market where every CHRO gets pitched dozens of HR platforms, you can't afford to waste Q1 on dead data.

HR leaders moved more in the last three years than ever before

The pandemic reshuffled everything. CHROs retired early. Directors of People moved to new companies. The entire function restructured multiple times. B2B contact data decays at roughly 25% per year, and in HR, that number spiked. Your database of HR buyers is likely more outdated than you think.

Titles in HR are wildly inconsistent

VP of People at one company is Chief People Officer at another. Director of Talent Acquisition could be VP of Recruiting somewhere else. HR Business Partner means different things at different companies. When your CRM has inconsistent titles, segmentation and routing break down.

Your SDRs are researchers, not sellers

Research shows sales reps spend only 30% of their time actually selling. The rest goes to data entry, research, and administrative work. Selling to HR requires understanding each company's tech stack, their current pain points, their buying process. When your data is bad, research time doubles.

HR Tech conferences generate fast-decaying leads

You spent $40K on that HR Tech Conference booth. You collected 400 badge scans. The average attendee probably changed jobs within two years. Without fast follow-up with clean data, that investment evaporates.

Every wrong phone number is a missed opportunity. Every bounced email is a competitor who got there first. Your team deserves better than a CRM full of job changers.
