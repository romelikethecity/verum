The HR Tech Lead Data Problem

You're selling HR software. HRIS, ATS, payroll, learning, performance management, people analytics. Your CRM has company names and maybe a general contact. That's not enough to run an effective sales motion.

HR buyer data is notoriously difficult. CHROs change roles frequently. HR team structures vary wildly by company. Employee headcount, the most important firmographic for HR tech, is often outdated or missing. Your SDRs spend hours researching each account before they can even start prospecting.

HR contacts are hard to find

Companies don't put their CHRO's direct line on the website. LinkedIn shows titles but not contact info. HR leaders are protective of their time because everyone is trying to sell them something. Without verified direct contact data, you're not breaking through.

Headcount data is stale

For HR tech sales, employee count determines everything: pricing tier, complexity, and whether they're even a fit. But the headcount in your CRM is from whenever the record was created. Companies grow, shrink, and change. You need current data.

HR org structures are inconsistent

Some companies have a CHRO. Some have a VP of People. Some have both. Some have distributed HR with leaders for recruiting, operations, and learning all reporting to different executives. You need to understand the buying committee, not just find a single contact.

Tech stack visibility is limited

What HRIS do they use? What ATS? Payroll? LMS? If you're selling HR tech, knowing their current stack is essential for competitive positioning and integration conversations. But companies don't advertise their HR tech stack.
