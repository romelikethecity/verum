The Travel Lead Data Problem

You're selling corporate travel services, hotel partnerships, airline contracts, or travel technology. Your CRM has company names and maybe a general contact. That's not enough to win corporate accounts or travel partnerships.

Travel buyer data is notoriously difficult. Corporate travel managers often have obscure titles. Decision-makers vary by company size. Office location data matters because travel patterns follow geography. Your SDRs spend hours researching each account before they can start prospecting.

Travel buyers are hard to identify

Some companies have dedicated travel managers. Others handle travel through HR, admin, or procurement. Smaller companies might have the CEO's assistant managing travel. Without knowing the right person and their title, you're guessing who to contact.

Company travel potential is unclear

How much does this company travel? You can't always tell from basic firmographics. A 500-person company with one office travels less than a 200-person consulting firm with distributed teams. You need signals about travel intensity, not just company size.

TMC relationships are invisible

Is this prospect already locked into a TMC contract? When does it renew? Who manages that relationship? Without this intelligence, you're pitching companies that aren't in-market or can't switch for another two years.

Location data is incomplete

Multi-location companies have complex travel patterns. Which offices are the biggest travel generators? Where are their remote employees? Office location data helps you understand travel needs and position your services geographically.
