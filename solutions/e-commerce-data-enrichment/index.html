The E-commerce Data Problem

It's Black Friday prep. Your marketing team is building the email campaigns that will drive half your Q4 revenue. Segmented lists, personalized offers, the whole playbook. Everything's ready.

The emails go out. Bounce rates come back at 18%. Your abandoned cart recovery sequence hits inboxes of people who already bought. Your "loyal customer" segment includes people who haven't purchased in three years. The personalization engine recommended winter coats to someone in Miami who just bought one.

Your customer database is technically full of data. It's just not accurate data.

Customer emails decay faster than you think

People change email addresses when they change jobs, switch providers, or just want a fresh start. Research shows email databases decay at roughly 25% per year. That promotional email you're crafting might be going to an inbox nobody checks anymore. Worse, high bounce rates hurt your sender reputation with ISPs.

One customer, five different records

Same person bought with their work email in 2021, personal email in 2022, and a typo'd version in 2023. They used three different shipping addresses. Your CDP sees three separate customers. Your lifetime value calculations are fiction. Your "new customer" promotions go to repeat buyers.

Addresses go stale silently

People move. A lot. About 10% of Americans change addresses every year. Your shipping address on file might be where they lived two apartments ago. Returns pile up. Packages get lost. Customer satisfaction tanks for reasons that have nothing to do with your product.

Segmentation built on bad data backfires

You can't personalize effectively when the underlying data is wrong. Recommending products based on outdated purchase history. Sending win-back campaigns to active customers. Offering first-time buyer discounts to your best customers. Every bad segment is money left on the table or, worse, customers actively annoyed.

Every undelivered email is a sale you didn't make. Every duplicate record is a customer you don't understand. Every wrong address is a package that won't arrive. Your competitors with clean data are converting at higher rates while you're troubleshooting deliverability.
