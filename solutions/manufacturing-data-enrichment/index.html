The Manufacturing Lead Data Problem

You're selling to manufacturers. OEMs, suppliers, job shops, industrial companies. Your CRM has company names and maybe a main office number. That's not enough to run an effective sales motion.

Manufacturing data is notoriously hard to get. Companies don't put their plant managers' direct lines online. Decision-makers vary by facility. Firmographic data like production capacity and certifications lives in scattered databases. Your SDRs spend hours researching each account before they can start prospecting.

Contacts are hidden

Manufacturing companies protect their operations teams. You might find a corporate directory, but good luck reaching the plant manager who actually makes equipment decisions. General numbers go to reception. Generic emails go nowhere. Without direct contact data, you're not getting through.

Plant vs corporate confusion

A manufacturer with 12 plants has 12 potential opportunities. But your CRM just shows the corporate HQ. You don't know which facilities are relevant, who runs each plant, or where decisions actually get made.

Firmographic data is scattered

Employee count, revenue, production capabilities, certifications, equipment types. The data that determines if a manufacturer is a fit for your product. This information exists but it's spread across industry databases, trade publications, and proprietary sources.

No visibility into technology

What ERP do they run? What PLM system? What automation equipment? If you're selling to manufacturing IT or operations, knowing their current stack is essential for qualification and personalization. But manufacturers don't advertise this.
