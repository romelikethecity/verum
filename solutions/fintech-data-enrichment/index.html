The Fintech Lead Data Problem

You're selling to banks, credit unions, insurance companies, wealth managers, payment processors. Financial institutions move slowly and require long, multi-threaded sales processes. You can't afford to waste cycles on incomplete data.

But financial services data is hard to get. Banks don't put their executives' direct lines on their websites. Fintech companies are notoriously private about their tech stacks. Credit unions have limited online presence. Your SDRs are spending hours researching each account before they can even start prospecting.

Contact data is buried

Financial institutions protect their executives. You might find a name and title, but good luck finding a direct email or phone number. Main lines go to gatekeepers. Generic emails go to spam folders. Without direct contact data, you're not getting through.

Organizational structures are complex

Banks have holding companies, subsidiaries, divisions, and regional structures. Who actually makes decisions? Is it at the bank level or the holding company? You need to map the buying committee across a complex org structure.

Firmographic data is scattered

Total assets, AUM, branch count, employee headcount, technology investments. The data points that determine if an institution is a fit. This information exists but it's spread across regulatory filings, press releases, and proprietary databases.

Tech stack visibility is limited

What core banking system do they run? What fraud detection? What digital banking platform? If you're selling technology to banks, knowing their current stack is essential for qualification and personalization. But banks don't advertise this.
