The Fintech Data Problem

It's Thursday afternoon. Your SDR is working a list of CFOs at mid-market companies. Perfect ICP. The kind of prospects who actually need your payment automation platform.

First call: "Sarah left six months ago." Second call: wrong number. Third call: the company was acquired, now part of a larger org with a completely different tech stack. Fourth call: finally connects, but they've already signed with a competitor. The deal was closed while your data was rotting.

In fintech, this happens constantly. The industry moves too fast for static data.

Your prospect data is decaying faster than you think

According to Landbase research, B2B contact databases experience decay rates between 22.5% and 70.3% annually, with email lists decaying at 28% per year. In fintech specifically, the churn is even worse. Job changes, layoffs, pivots, acquisitions. By the time your SDR calls that "fresh" lead, there's a decent chance the person doesn't work there anymore.

Data silos are killing your visibility

In most fintech companies, data lives in at least three places that don't talk to each other. Your CRM, your payment platform, and your product analytics. Maybe more if you're doing compliance reporting. Each system has its own version of the truth. Your sales team sees one picture. Finance sees another. Nobody trusts the numbers.

The cost adds up fast

Gartner research indicates that organizations lose an average of $12.9 million per year due to poor data quality. Targeting errors. Wasted outreach. Compliance issues from sending communications to the wrong contacts. Missed opportunities because your data said a company wasn't a fit when it actually was.

High churn makes everything harder

B2B fintech companies report roughly 26% annual churn. That's the highest of any B2B tech vertical. Your customers are leaving, your prospects are changing jobs, and the market is consolidating. Standing still means falling behind.

Every bad data point is a wasted call, a bounced email, or worse, a compliance risk. Your reps deserve better than spending half their day verifying information that should already be accurate.
