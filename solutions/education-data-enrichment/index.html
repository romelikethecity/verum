The Education Data Problem

It's annual fund season. Your development team is preparing the big fall appeal. Direct mail to 50,000 alumni. Email campaigns. Phonathon scripts. The goal is ambitious but achievable. If people actually get your messages.

Last year's appeal came back with 4,000 undeliverable mailers. The email campaign had a 22% bounce rate. The phonathon team spent more time listening to disconnected number messages than talking to actual alumni. Your "lost alumni" file keeps growing.

Meanwhile, you know there are successful graduates out there who would give if you could just reach them.

Alumni scatter after graduation

Students leave with their college email and maybe a home address. Within a year, both are probably outdated. Within five years, they've moved twice, changed jobs three times, and gotten married (or divorced). The contact info in your database reflects who they were at 22, not who they are now.

Decades of accumulated data decay

A university with 40 years of alumni has records that span the landline era to the smartphone age. Some records have addresses but no email. Others have email but the person hasn't checked that inbox since Gmail launched. Contact data decays at roughly 30% per year. Multiply that across decades and the problem compounds.

Major gift prospects hide in bad data

Your highest potential donors might be sitting in your database with wrong contact info. That alumnus who just made partner at a major firm? You're still sending mail to their parents' house. The entrepreneur who sold a company? You don't even know they changed industries. Wealth is meaningless without reachability.

Enrollment marketing needs accurate prospect data too

It's not just alumni. Prospective student data goes stale fast. High schoolers change their minds, move, get new phone numbers. The inquiry you got in September might be unreachable by April decision time. Every unreachable prospect is an enrollment opportunity lost.

Every undeliverable appeal is money you didn't raise. Every bounced email is an engagement opportunity missed. Every disconnected phone number is an alumnus who might have given if you'd just reached them. Your competitors with clean data are raising more money with smaller lists.

Three fundamental differences:

Different problem. ZoomInfo sells B2B contact databases. If you need net-new corporate contacts, that's their strength. But if you already have decades of alumni data that needs cleaning? That's us. We fix what you have.

Different pricing. ZoomInfo charges recurring annual licenses, typically $15K-$50K+ per year. We charge per project. Clean your data before the capital campaign, pay for that project.

We work with directory information and publicly available data. We don't access protected educational records. Encrypted transfers, no data sharing with third parties. Happy to discuss specific compliance requirements.

We work with community colleges and major research universities. Data quality challenges are similar regardless of enrollment. An alumni database with 10,000 records has the same decay issues as one with 500,000.
