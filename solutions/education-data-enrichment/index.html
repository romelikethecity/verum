The Education Lead Data Problem

You're selling edtech, curriculum, services, or products to schools and universities. Your CRM has institution names and maybe a main office number. That's not enough to run an effective education sales motion.

Education buyer data is challenging. Administrator turnover is high. Decision-making authority varies by institution. Budget cycles and procurement processes differ. Your SDRs spend hours researching each school before they can start prospecting.

Administrators are hard to reach

Principals and department heads are busy. School office staff screens calls. District offices protect superintendent time. Without direct contact information, you're not reaching the decision-makers who evaluate and purchase.

Institution data is incomplete

Enrollment size matters for pricing and fit. Budget data indicates purchasing capacity. Title I status signals funding availability. But this institution-level data isn't in your CRM.

Decision-makers vary by institution

At small schools, the principal decides. At larger schools, there are department heads and coordinators. At districts, there are central office administrators. Understanding who makes decisions for your product type at each institution requires research.

Technology and curriculum data is hidden

What LMS do they use? What curriculum? If you're selling edtech, knowing their current stack is essential for qualification and competitive positioning. But schools don't advertise their technology choices.
