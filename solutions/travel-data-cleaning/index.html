The Travel Data Quality Problem

You're running a hotel, airline, OTA, or travel service. Your PMS and CRM hold years of guest data from dozens of booking channels. But that data has become fragmented and unreliable.

Travel data degrades fast. Guests change email addresses, switch jobs (killing corporate contact info), and book through different channels each trip. Loyalty programs create one profile, OTAs create another, and direct bookings create a third. Your team can't deliver personalized experiences when they don't know who's actually walking through the door.

Guests exist multiple times

The same traveler booked through Expedia last year, your direct site this quarter, and called reservations yesterday. Now you have three profiles with different information. Your loyalty program shows conflicting point balances. Your front desk can't see their full history. Personalization fails.

Contact information is outdated

Email addresses change. Phone numbers update. Corporate travelers switch companies. Your pre-arrival emails bounce. Your loyalty communications don't reach members. You're marketing to addresses that haven't worked in years.

Corporate accounts are stale

If you sell to corporate travel managers, your B2B data has the same problems. The travel buyer who signed your contract moved on. The corporate rate contact changed departments. You're maintaining relationships with people who can't actually make purchasing decisions anymore.

Property and partner data is inconsistent

For hotel groups and OTAs, property data is another challenge. The same hotel appears under different names. DMC partners have inconsistent contact records. Travel agent accounts are duplicated. Your operational efficiency suffers.
