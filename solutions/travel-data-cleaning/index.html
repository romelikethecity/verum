The Travel & Hospitality Sales Data Problem

You sell technology, services, or partnerships to travel and hospitality companies. Your CRM contains hotel chains, airlines, travel agencies, tour operators, and OTAs. But the travel industry has been through massive disruption.

That hotel you've been targeting? Changed brand flags last quarter. The GM you built a relationship with? Moved to a different property. The travel agency on your target list? Closed permanently after industry downturns. Your database reflects a travel landscape that no longer exists.

Travel and hospitality data has unique challenges. High employee turnover, property rebranding, and the complexity of ownership versus management structures mean your prospect data goes stale faster than most industries.

Properties change brands constantly

Hotels switch brand affiliations, join or leave franchise systems, and rebrand after ownership changes. That Marriott in your database might now be a Hilton—or an independent. Your target account list includes properties under names that no longer exist, making your outreach look uninformed.

Hospitality turnover is extreme

The travel industry has some of the highest turnover rates of any sector. General managers move between properties. Corporate executives change companies. The decision-maker you've been cultivating might be at a completely different company than your CRM shows.

Ownership vs. management complexity

Who actually makes purchasing decisions? The property owner? The management company? The brand? The same property might have different entities handling different decisions. Your data doesn't capture who controls what.

Purchased lists compound the problem

You've bought hotel databases, airline contact lists, travel agency directories. Each list has different naming conventions, different ages, different accuracy levels. Now you have the same property or company five times with conflicting information.

Clean data is essential for travel sales. An industry still recovering from disruption has no patience for vendors who don't know their current situation. You can't afford to waste time pursuing closed properties or departed contacts.
