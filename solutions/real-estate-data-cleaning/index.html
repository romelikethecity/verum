The Real Estate Sales Data Problem

You sell software, services, or marketing to real estate companies. Your CRM contains brokerages, property management firms, commercial real estate companies, and developers. But the real estate industry is in constant motion.

That top agent you've been targeting? Switched brokerages last quarter. The property management firm you built a relationship with? Acquired by a larger player. The broker on your target list? Retired from the business. Your database reflects a real estate landscape that no longer exists.

Real estate data has unique challenges. High agent turnover, brokerage consolidation, and market-driven changes mean your prospect data goes stale faster than most industries.

Agents switch brokerages constantly

Real estate agents move between brokerages regularly, driven by commission splits, branding, and market conditions. The agent or team you've been targeting might be at a completely different brokerage than your CRM shows. Your outreach hits old email addresses and goes unanswered.

Brokerage consolidation reshapes the market

Independent brokerages join franchise networks. Franchise offices change hands. National brands acquire regional players. Your target account list includes brokerages that no longer exist independently or have changed ownership structures.

Market conditions drive exits

Challenging markets push agents out of the business. Brokerages close offices or shut down entirely. The database you built during a hot market includes agents who've left real estate and companies that no longer operate.

Purchased lists compound the problem

You've bought agent databases, brokerage lists, property manager directories. Each list has different naming conventions, different ages, different accuracy levels. Now you have the same agent five times with different brokerages listed.

Clean data is essential for real estate sales. A fragmented industry with high turnover means you can't afford to waste time pursuing agents who left or brokerages that closed.
