The AdTech Data Quality Problem

Your programmatic team builds audience segments. Job titles, company sizes, industries. The targeting looks precise in the DMP. Then the campaign launches and performance disappoints.

Someone pulls a sample. Half the records are duplicates inflating your audience counts. A third of the job titles are outdated. Email formats are inconsistent across systems. The whole foundation was dirty before a single impression was served.

This is the adtech data quality crisis that nobody wants to talk about.

Duplicates inflate your audience counts

The same person exists in your system three, five, sometimes ten times. Different email variations. Slightly different name spellings. Your reach calculations are fiction. Your frequency caps don't work because the system doesn't know it's the same person. You're serving the same ads repeatedly to the same people and calling it scale.

Format inconsistencies break your targeting

One system stores phone numbers as (555) 123-4567. Another uses 5551234567. A third has +1-555-123-4567. Same story with addresses, company names, job titles. When formats don't match, your identity resolution fails. Your segments miss people who should be included. Your exclusions don't work.

Invalid data wastes impressions

Emails that bounce. Phone numbers that don't exist. Companies that went out of business. Every record that shouldn't be in your audience is money spent targeting nobody. The CPMs are the same whether the data is valid or not.

Data sprawl across systems compounds the mess

DMP, CDP, CRM, email platform, analytics tools. Each system has its own version of the truth. None of them match. When you try to build cross-channel audiences, you're combining dirty data with more dirty data. The errors multiply.

Clean data isn't a nice-to-have in adtech. It's the foundation everything else depends on. Dirty data means wasted spend, wrong targeting, and campaigns that underperform no matter how good your creative is.
