The AdTech Data Quality Problem

Advertising technology companies deal with some of the messiest data in B2B. You're selling to agencies with complex holding company structures. Brands with constantly changing marketing teams. Media buyers who change jobs every 18 months. And everyone has been touched by multiple vendors, events, and sales efforts.

AdTech data has unique challenges. Agency hierarchies are confusing. The same agency might appear under its own name, its network name, and its holding company name. Media buyers move so frequently that half your contacts are at different agencies than your records show. And the advertising industry's constant M&A creates orphaned records everywhere.

Agency hierarchies are a mess

"Mindshare" and "GroupM" and "WPP" might all appear in your CRM for the same relationship. Is it three separate accounts or one? Your ABM campaigns break when you can't accurately count coverage at each agency or holding company.

Media buyers move constantly

A media buyer at Horizon today might be at an in-house brand team next month and at a different agency next year. Your database is full of people at the wrong companies, and you're emailing them at addresses that bounce.

Brand marketing teams churn

CMOs average 40 months in role. Media directors even less. The brand contacts you built relationships with are gone, but your CRM still shows them there. Every bounce and wrong-person response hurts your reputation.

Duplicates from events and outreach

Advertising Week. Cannes Lions. DMEXCO. Every event creates a batch of leads that overlap with your existing database. Now you have the same media planner listed three times with different information from each event.
