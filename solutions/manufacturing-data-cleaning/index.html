The Manufacturing Data Quality Problem

You're managing relationships with hundreds of vendors, suppliers, and distributors. Your CRM and ERP systems hold critical contact and company data. But over time, that data has degraded into chaos.

Manufacturing has unique data challenges. Suppliers get acquired. Plants close or relocate. Procurement contacts change frequently. And the same vendor might be in your system under their corporate name, a division name, a DBA, and a plant-specific entry. Your team wastes time figuring out which record is current instead of actually getting work done.

Vendor names are inconsistent

"Acme Manufacturing" and "Acme Mfg" and "Acme Mfg Co" and "Acme Manufacturing LLC" are the same company in your database. Maybe. Or they're different divisions. When you can't trust vendor names, spend analysis fails and supplier consolidation becomes guesswork.

Contacts are outdated

The sales rep you worked with two years ago has moved on. The plant manager retired. The procurement contact switched to a competitor. Manufacturing has high turnover in key roles, and every job change creates a stale record that wastes your team's time when they try to reach out.

Plant and location data is wrong

Suppliers open new facilities, close old ones, and consolidate operations. Your database still shows the plant that closed last year. Your logistics team plans around facilities that don't exist anymore. Accurate location data matters for supply chain planning.

Duplicates create procurement confusion

When the same supplier exists multiple times with different information, which record has the current pricing? Which one has the correct payment terms? Purchasing teams make decisions based on incomplete or conflicting data, and that costs money.
