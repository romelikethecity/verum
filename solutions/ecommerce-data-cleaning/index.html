The E-commerce B2B Data Problem

You sell tools, services, or platforms to e-commerce companies. Your CRM is stuffed with Shopify merchants, DTC brands, and online retailers. But when your sales team actually tries to use this data, reality hits.

Half those hot DTC brands from 2021? Gone or pivoted. The founder you've been nurturing? Left to start something new. That e-commerce company that looked perfect? Acquired and absorbed. Your reps are chasing ghosts while real opportunities slip by.

E-commerce data is uniquely volatile. And most B2B teams selling into this space just accept the mess.

E-commerce companies fail or pivot constantly

The DTC boom created thousands of brands. Many didn't survive. Others pivoted to wholesale or got acquired. Your prospect database is full of companies that no longer exist in the form you recorded. Your reps waste time researching only to find dead ends.

Founders and leaders move fast

E-commerce leadership is transient. Founders sell and move on. Marketing directors hop between brands. The decision-maker you targeted six months ago might be three companies removed by now. Your outreach hits old email addresses while the real opportunity is elsewhere.

Purchased lists compound the problem

You've bought Shopify merchant lists, e-commerce brand databases, DTC company data. Each list adds duplicates. Same company, different spellings. Same person, different roles. Your CRM becomes a maze of conflicting records that nobody trusts.

Company data goes stale quickly

That fast-growing brand you flagged as a target? They might have been acquired, changed names, or shut down their e-commerce operations. E-commerce moves fast. Your static database can't keep up. Targeting becomes guesswork.

When you're selling to e-commerce, bad data directly costs you deals. Your competitors with clean, current data are already in conversations while your team researches whether prospects still exist.
