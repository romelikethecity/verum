The E-commerce Data Quality Problem

You're running an online store or D2C brand. Your database holds years of customer transactions, email signups, and loyalty program data. But that data has become fragmented and unreliable.

E-commerce data degrades fast. Customers use different emails, checkout as guests, and create new accounts when they forget passwords. Marketing campaigns generate leads that never convert. Email addresses go stale. Your customer lifetime value calculations are wrong because the same person appears as multiple customers.

Customers exist multiple times

john@gmail.com placed an order last year. john.smith@gmail.com checked out as a guest. J Smith used a work email for a different order. Now you have three customers who are actually one person. Your email campaigns hit them multiple times. Your loyalty program shows three small accounts instead of one valuable customer.

Guest checkouts create chaos

Every guest checkout is a potential duplicate. Customers who forget their passwords check out as guests. Your database grows with records that should be consolidated with existing accounts. True customer count and CLV become impossible to measure accurately.

Email addresses go bad

People change email providers, abandon old addresses, and let inboxes fill up. Your marketing emails bounce. Your abandoned cart sequences don't reach customers. Your email reputation suffers from sending to invalid addresses.

Vendor and supplier data is messy

If you work with vendors, suppliers, or wholesale accounts, that B2B data has its own problems. Company names are inconsistent. Contacts have changed. Your vendor management becomes difficult when you can't trust the underlying data.
