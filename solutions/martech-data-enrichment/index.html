The MarTech Data Problem

It's pipeline review. Your SDR team is working through a list of marketing leaders at mid-market companies. Perfect ICP. Great intent signals. Should be solid meetings.

Except the CMO at target account #1 left for a startup three months ago. The VP of Demand Gen at #2 got laid off in the last round of cuts. The Director of Marketing Ops at #3 is now the CMO somewhere else. Your "hot leads" are sending emails to people who won't see them.

Marketing leadership turns over faster than almost any other function. Your data can't keep up.

Marketing executives move constantly

CMO tenure averages around 18 months. VP and Director level marketing roles aren't much more stable. Budget cuts, reorganizations, pivots to "growth" structures. The marketing leader you identified six months ago has a significant chance of being somewhere else today.

The martech landscape creates complexity

Over 11,000 marketing technology solutions exist. Your prospects are already using some of them. But your CRM doesn't tell you which ones. You're pitching blind, not knowing if they're already using a competitor or a complementary tool you could integrate with.

Company data fragments across systems

Your prospects have marketing data scattered everywhere. CRM, marketing automation, analytics, data warehouse. Each system has different contact information, different company hierarchies, different account statuses. You're selling to companies you don't fully understand.

SDR research time kills productivity

Every hour your SDRs spend researching whether a contact is still at a company is an hour not spent on outreach. Manual verification doesn't scale. But bad data tanks reply rates and damages sender reputation.

Every email to someone who left is a missed opportunity. Every call to a disconnected number is time wasted. Every prospect whose tech stack you don't know is a pitch that might miss the mark. In a crowded martech market, you can't afford to be working from yesterday's data.
