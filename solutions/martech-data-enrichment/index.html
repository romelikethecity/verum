The MarTech Lead Data Problem

You're selling marketing technology. Automation, analytics, attribution, personalization. Your buyers are marketing leaders who get pitched constantly. Generic outreach doesn't work. You need precise targeting and relevant positioning.

MarTech buyer data has unique challenges. Marketing teams are bombarded by vendors. Contact information is protected. Marketing tech stacks are complex and constantly changing. And the signals that indicate buying readiness, like contract renewals and dissatisfaction with current tools, aren't publicly visible. Your SDRs spend hours researching each account.

Marketing leaders are hard to reach

CMOs and VPs of Marketing get hundreds of vendor pitches. They're protective of their time and contact information. Without verified direct contact data and a relevant angle, you're not getting through the noise.

Buying committees are large

A marketing platform purchase might involve the CMO, VP of Demand Gen, Marketing Ops lead, IT for integrations, and finance for budget approval. You need the full buying committee, not just a single contact.

Tech stack determines positioning

If you're selling marketing tech, knowing their current stack is essential. Are they on HubSpot or Marketo? What CRM? What CDP? This context determines competitive positioning and integration messaging.

Marketing investment varies wildly

Some companies invest heavily in marketing. Others have minimal budgets. You need signals that indicate marketing investment: team size, ad spend, active campaigns. Without these signals, you're qualifying blind.
