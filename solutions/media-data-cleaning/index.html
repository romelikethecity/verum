The Media Sales Data Problem

You sell technology, services, or advertising to media and entertainment companies. Your CRM contains publishers, streaming platforms, studios, and content creators. But the media industry is in constant upheaval.

That digital publisher you've been pursuing? Laid off half their staff and pivoted to video. The entertainment exec you built a relationship with? Moved to a streaming competitor. The publication on your target list? Shut down entirely. Your database reflects a media landscape that no longer exists.

Media data has unique challenges. Industry restructuring, constant M&A, and workforce volatility mean your prospect data ages faster than almost any other sector.

Media consolidation reshapes the landscape

Publishers merge. Studios get acquired. Streaming platforms combine or launch new ones. The media company you've been targeting might now be part of a larger conglomerate with different decision-makers. Your target account list includes properties that no longer exist independently.

Media contacts move constantly

Layoffs, restructuring, and aggressive poaching mean media executives change jobs frequently. The decision-maker you've been nurturing might be two companies removed from where your CRM says they are. Entire teams get disbanded and reformed elsewhere.

Publications and properties launch and fold

New streaming services launch. Digital publications shut down. Content studios spin up and dissolve. Your database includes media properties that no longer exist while missing new entrants that should be targets.

Purchased lists compound the problem

You've bought media databases, publisher lists, entertainment executive contacts. Each list has different naming conventions, different ages, different accuracy levels. Now you have the same company five times with conflicting information.

Clean data is essential for media sales. Tight budgets and competitive pressure mean you can't afford to waste time pursuing companies that shuttered or contacts who moved.
