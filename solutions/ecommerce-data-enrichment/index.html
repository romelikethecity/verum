The E-commerce Lead Data Problem

You're selling to e-commerce brands, D2C companies, or retailers with online operations. Your CRM has company names and maybe a website. That's not enough to run an effective sales motion.

E-commerce company data is fragmented. D2C brands are often small and private, making firmographics hard to find. Decision-makers have varied titles. Platform and tech stack information requires technical detection. Your SDRs spend hours researching each prospect before they can start outreach.

Decision-makers are hard to identify

Who owns e-commerce at this company? Is it a VP of E-commerce, Head of Digital, or the CMO? For smaller D2C brands, the founder might be making all decisions. Without knowing the right contact, you're guessing who to reach.

Company data is incomplete

D2C brands don't publish revenue. Employee counts are often unavailable for smaller companies. You can't tell if this is a $1M startup or a $50M growth brand without research. Basic qualification becomes time-consuming.

Platform and tech stack is hidden

Are they on Shopify or Magento? What marketing tools do they use? For e-commerce platform vendors and app developers, understanding the current tech stack is essential for qualification and positioning. But detecting this requires technical analysis.

Growth signals are invisible

Which e-commerce brands are scaling? Who just raised funding? Which companies are hiring aggressively? Growth signals indicate buying potential, but they're not in your CRM.
