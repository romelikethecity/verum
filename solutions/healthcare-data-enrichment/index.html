The Healthcare Data Gap Problem

You're selling to healthcare organizations. You know your target accounts. But your CRM has a hospital name and maybe a general phone number. That's not enough to run a real sales motion.

Healthcare buying committees are complex. The CIO cares about integration. The CMIO cares about clinical workflow. The CFO cares about ROI. You need to reach all of them, and you need accurate contact data to do it. Most teams are working with incomplete records that force reps to spend hours hunting for the right person.

Contact data is sparse

Hospital websites list a main phone number and maybe an executive team page with no contact details. LinkedIn shows who works there but not how to reach them. Your SDRs are spending half their time researching instead of selling.

Organizational data is incomplete

Is that hospital part of a health system? How many beds? What EHR are they running? These details matter for targeting and personalization, but they're scattered across dozens of sources that nobody has time to compile manually.

Provider data changes constantly

Physicians move between organizations. Administrators get promoted or leave. Health systems acquire new facilities. The contact you found last quarter might not be there anymore, and new decision-makers have appeared that you don't know about.

NPI data needs context

You might have NPI numbers, but do you have the associated contact data? NPI tells you someone is a licensed provider, but it doesn't give you their current email, their organization's bed count, or whether they're the right person to talk to about your solution.
