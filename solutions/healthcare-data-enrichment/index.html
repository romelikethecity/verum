The Healthcare Data Problem

It's Wednesday morning. Your medical device rep is driving to a hospital for what should be an easy upsell. She checked Salesforce before leaving. Dr. Martinez, the orthopedic surgeon who loved your last demo, is her contact.

She gets to the hospital. Dr. Martinez left eight months ago. Moved to a competing health system across town. The new surgeon hasn't heard of your company. The visit is wasted. The drive was wasted. And somewhere in your CRM, Dr. Martinez's old record is still marked as "active."

This happens more in healthcare than any other industry. The data just moves faster.

Provider data decays at terrifying speed

Almost one-third of physicians change affiliations and practice locations every year. A former COO of a national health plan estimated that 6 to 8 percent of their network provider information was changing monthly. Your CRM can't keep up. Nobody's can, without active maintenance.

The NPI registry is a mess

According to Curatus research, only 8.2% of the 6.2 million NPIs in the NPPES database have been updated within the past year. More than half haven't been touched in over five years. By law, providers are supposed to update within 30 days of any change. In practice, they don't. That NPI you're relying on for credentialing or claims? There's a good chance the address is wrong.

Directory accuracy is worse than you think

A study published in PMC found that all specialties had less than 50% consistency of physician addresses and phone numbers across health insurer directories. Not some specialties. All of them. Your internal data probably isn't much better.

Specialty matters, and not in a good way

Primary care physicians and high-referral specialties like dermatology tend to have better data consistency. But anesthesiologists, radiologists, emergency medicine docs? Their data is all over the place because they rarely get direct patient referrals. If you're selling to hospitals or health systems, you need all of them accurate.

The cost of this chaos shows up everywhere. Wasted sales visits. Denied claims from incorrect NPI data. Marketing campaigns sent to physicians who left years ago. Credentialing delays because the addresses don't match. And your team spending hours every week trying to verify what should already be clean.
