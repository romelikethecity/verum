The Insurance Sales Data Problem

You sell technology, services, or capacity to insurance companies. Your CRM contains carriers, agencies, MGAs, and brokers. But the insurance industry is consolidating faster than ever.

That independent agency you've been nurturing? Acquired by a PE-backed aggregator last month. The agency principal you built a relationship with? Cashed out and retired. The MGA on your target list? Merged with a competitor. Your database reflects an insurance distribution landscape that no longer exists.

Insurance data has unique challenges. Record agency M&A activity, carrier restructuring, and the complexity of distribution relationships mean your prospect data ages fast.

Agency consolidation is relentless

Hundreds of agency M&A deals happen every year. Independent agencies join aggregators. Regional brokers get acquired by nationals. Your target account list includes agencies that no longer exist independently. Decision-making shifts to new ownership. The principal you knew might not have authority anymore.

Insurance contacts move frequently

Agency principals sell and move on. Carrier executives rotate between companies. Underwriters shift between MGAs. The decision-maker you've been nurturing might be two companies removed from where your CRM says they are.

Company structures are complex

Is it a carrier, an MGA, an agency, or a cluster? Parent companies, affiliates, and program administrators create confusion. The same organization might appear multiple times under different names. Your data doesn't reflect the actual business relationships.

Purchased lists compound the problem

You've bought agency databases, carrier contact lists, MGA directories. Each list has different naming conventions, different ages, different levels of accuracy. Now you have the same company five times with conflicting information.

Clean data is essential for insurance sales. Long sales cycles and relationship-driven selling mean you can't afford to waste time pursuing agencies that were acquired or contacts who retired.
