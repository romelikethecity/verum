The Logistics Data Quality Problem

You're running a brokerage, carrier, or shipping operation. Your TMS and CRM hold years of carrier relationships, shipper accounts, and lane history. But that data has become fragmented and unreliable.

Logistics has unique data challenges. Carriers operate under different DBAs. Owner-operators get acquired by larger fleets. Operating authority changes. Contacts at shippers turn over frequently. And every new load adds records that may duplicate existing relationships.

Carriers are duplicated everywhere

"ABC Trucking" and "ABC Transport LLC" and "ABC Logistics" might all be the same carrier. Or they might be different companies. When you're booking loads, you can't easily see a carrier's full lane history because it's scattered across duplicate records.

Contacts are constantly moving

The dispatcher you worked with last quarter moved to another carrier. The shipper's logistics manager changed companies. Freight is a high-turnover industry, and every job change creates stale contact data.

Authority and insurance data is stale

Operating authority changes. Insurance certificates expire. Your approved carrier list shows credentials that were current months ago. Compliance requirements mean you need current data, but manual verification is time-consuming.

Shipper data is inconsistent

The same shipper might appear under corporate name, division name, and facility-specific entries. Location data is scattered. Your account-level view of shipper relationships is fragmented.
