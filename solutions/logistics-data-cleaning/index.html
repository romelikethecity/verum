The Logistics Sales Data Problem

You sell technology, services, or capacity to the logistics industry. Your CRM contains shippers, carriers, 3PLs, freight brokers, and warehousing companies. But the logistics industry is volatile.

That trucking company you've been pursuing? Went out of business during the freight recession. The 3PL you built a relationship with? Acquired by a larger player. The supply chain director on your target list? Moved to a competitor. Your database reflects a logistics landscape that no longer exists.

Logistics data has unique challenges. Carrier turnover, constant M&A, and the cyclical nature of the freight market mean your prospect data ages faster than you'd expect.

Carrier volatility is extreme

Trucking companies enter and exit the market constantly. Small carriers go out of business. Others grow rapidly through acquisition. Your carrier database includes companies that no longer operate and misses new entrants that should be targets.

3PL and broker consolidation continues

PE firms continue rolling up freight brokers and 3PLs. Independent brokerages join larger platforms. Your target account list includes companies that no longer exist independently. Decision-making shifts to new corporate structures.

Supply chain contacts move frequently

VP of Supply Chain, Director of Logistics, Transportation Managerâ€”these roles are in high demand and turn over frequently. The decision-maker you've been nurturing might be at a different company than your CRM shows.

Purchased lists compound the problem

You've bought shipper databases, carrier lists, 3PL directories. Each list has different naming conventions, different ages, different levels of accuracy. Now you have the same company five times with conflicting information.

Clean data is essential for logistics sales. When market conditions are tough, you can't afford to waste time pursuing companies that closed or contacts who moved.
