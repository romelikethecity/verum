The Retail Lead Data Problem

You're selling to retailers. Products, technology, services, or solutions for retail operations. Your CRM has company names and maybe a corporate contact. That's not enough to run an effective sales motion.

Retail buyer data is hard to find. Merchandising and buying teams are protected from vendors. Corporate offices screen calls. Store-level contacts are scattered. Your SDRs spend hours researching each retailer before they can start prospecting.

Buyers are hard to reach

Retail buyers get pitched constantly. They don't list direct contact information. Corporate switchboards don't connect you. Without verified direct contact data, you're not breaking through to the people who make purchasing decisions.

Store footprint is unclear

How many stores do they have? Where are they located? Are they expanding or contracting? Store count and format data determines deal size and fit, but it's not in your CRM.

Technology stack is hidden

What POS system do they use? What e-commerce platform? If you're selling retail technology, knowing their current stack is essential for qualification and competitive positioning. But retailers don't advertise their infrastructure.

Expansion signals are invisible

Which retailers are opening new stores? Who just raised capital? Which chains are investing in new categories? Growth signals indicate opportunity, but they're not in your CRM.
