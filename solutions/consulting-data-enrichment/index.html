The Consulting Firm Data Problem

It's the start of proposal season. Your practice lead is putting together a pitch for a major transformation project. She needs to understand the relationship history. Who at the firm knows who at the client? What work have we done before? Who's the sponsor we should approach?

She opens the CRM. Three records for the target company, each with different contacts. The CFO listed retired two years ago. The VP of Operations moved to a competitor. The Chief Transformation Officer? Email bounces. Phone disconnected.

Two hours later, she's cross-referenced LinkedIn, bothered three partners who might have connections, and built a spreadsheet she still doesn't fully trust. The pitch deadline is tomorrow.

Client contacts move constantly

Research shows 30% of contact data degrades every year. In consulting's target market of C-suite and senior executives, that number runs even higher. The sponsor who hired you for last year's project? They've probably been promoted, moved companies, or restructured out. Your CRM doesn't update itself.

Partners spend hours hunting for data instead of developing business

Validity research found workers spend 13 hours per week just hunting for basic information in their CRM. For consulting partners billing at $400/hour, that's $270,000 annually per partner in lost opportunity cost. Time that should go to client development goes to data archaeology.

Relationship intelligence is scattered everywhere

Some relationships live in the CRM. Some in partners' personal Outlook contacts. Some in the heads of long-tenured staff. Some in the billing system. Getting a complete picture of client relationships means pulling from five different sources and hoping they match.

Cross-sell opportunities hide in bad data

You know there's more business in existing clients. Every firm does. But pulling together engagement history, contact relationships, and service gaps across systems takes so long that opportunities go to competitors while you're still researching.

Every wrong phone number is a cold call that should have been warm. Every bounced email is a relationship that's drifting away. Your partners deserve better than a CRM full of decay.

Common Questions About Consulting Data Enrichment

There isn't really an "implementation." Send us a file, we send back clean data. First project typically completes in 24-48 hours. Ongoing integrations take a bit longer to set up, but we're talking days, not months.

Can you track when our client contacts change roles?

Yes. We flag job changes proactively. Know when a sponsor gets promoted, moves companies, or leaves the role. This lets you follow relationships instead of losing them, and identify when past contacts become new opportunity sources.

Does this work with our CRM?

Almost certainly. We've worked with Salesforce, Microsoft Dynamics, HubSpot, and most other CRM platforms used by consulting firms. If your system can export data, we can clean it. If you want cleaned data pushed back in, we can work with your IT team on that too.

Three fundamental differences:

Different problem. ZoomInfo sells access to their contact database. If you need net-new prospect names, that's their strength. But if you already have decades of client relationships and prospect data, and now that data is a mess? That's us. We clean and organize what you have.

Different pricing. ZoomInfo charges recurring annual licenses, typically $15K-$50K+ per year. We charge per project. Clean your data once, pay once. Need a refresh in six months? Pay for that refresh.

Different ownership. ZoomInfo's licensing terms require you to delete their data if you cancel. With Verum, your cleaned data is yours forever.

What about confidentiality?

We work with contact and company information, not engagement details or deliverables. Encrypted transfers, NDAs available, deletion after project completion unless you request otherwise. We understand consulting confidentiality requirements.

Do you work with firms of our size?

We work with boutique firms and global consultancies. Data problems don't discriminate by headcount. A CRM with 5,000 contacts has the same decay issues as one with 500,000.
