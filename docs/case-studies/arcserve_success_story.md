# How We Turned 50,000 Rows of Trash Data Into a Revenue-Generating Machine

## The Problem (Or: Why Your CRM Probably Looks Like This Too)

Here's the thing about enterprise CRM data—it rots.

Not like, metaphorically. I mean it actually degrades over time, getting worse and worse until you're basically operating with a database full of digital compost. And that's exactly what we found when a data protection company came to us.

They had 50,000 accounts and contacts. Sounds impressive, right?

Wrong.

This was 17+ years of accumulated mess. Every merger, every acquisition, every lazy data entry from a tired SDR at 4:47pm on a Friday. All of it, just... sitting there. Festering.

**Here's what we're talking about:**

The industry classifications? Missing for thousands of accounts. Employee counts—one of the most basic data points you'd want for ABM targeting—missing for nearly 10% of the database. Revenue data was all over the place. Some records in thousands, some in millions, some just... blank.

And the contacts?

Oh man, the contacts. We're talking records that hadn't been touched in years. People who'd retired. People who'd changed companies three times. People who'd moved from IT into marketing into sales and were now running a bakery in Vermont. (Okay, maybe not that last one, but you get the idea.)

**But here's where it gets expensive:**

They had 703 MSP accounts—managed service providers—buried in generic "Partner" categories. For a data protection company, MSPs aren't just customers. They're force multipliers. One MSP can deploy your solution across dozens or hundreds of end clients.

And they had no idea these accounts existed.

They couldn't tell partners from end customers from distributors. No visibility into which companies were actually subsidiaries of bigger fish. Turns out, 305 subsidiaries were hiding across 122 parent companies. So you'd have reps accidentally pitching different divisions of the same organization, looking like idiots in the process.

Technology stack data? Non-existent. They had no clue what tools their prospects were using, what competitors they might be replacing, nothing.

**The operational nightmare:**

Duplicate records everywhere. Both the obvious ones (literally the same company entered twice) and the sneaky ones (Microsoft Corp vs. Microsoft Corporation vs. MSFT vs. that one rep who just put "Microsoft").

Sales reps were spending 30-45 minutes per account just doing basic research before they could even think about outreach. 

Marketing was firing campaigns at the wrong people with the wrong messaging.

Deal velocity was suffering because nobody could quickly identify who the actual decision-maker was.

And then there was the formatting disaster—89,165 cells across their database needed manual cleanup. Bad formats, inconsistent data entry, null values that broke their CRM workflows.

The real cost? Their sales and marketing teams were flying blind. They couldn't segment. Couldn't prioritize. Couldn't do anything except burn time on manual research that should've been automated ages ago.

## What We Did (The Systematic Approach)

Look, we could've just thrown some data at them and called it a day.

We didn't.

What they needed was a complete overhaul—not a band-aid, but surgery. Methodical, comprehensive, and built to actually drive revenue.

**Account Intelligence From Scratch:**

We took 1,189 accounts and ran them through a multi-layered enrichment process. Validated legal entity names. Standardized everything—company names, domains, websites. (You'd be shocked at how many variations of "Microsoft" exist in the wild. I mean genuinely shocked.)

Enriched all the missing stuff: industry, employee size, revenue, headquarters location.

But here's the key part—we didn't just fill in blanks. We classified every single account by type. Partners. End Customers. Distributors. MSPs. OEMs. Real classifications that sales and marketing could actually use to segment and target properly.

Oh, and technology stacks. We discovered what backup vendors they were using, their cloud providers, their security tools. The kind of intelligence that lets you walk into a conversation knowing exactly what you're competing against or complementing.

**Contact Data That's Actually Useful:**

For 1,000 contacts, we went deep. Found 525 LinkedIn profiles to verify current employment. Caught 195 job changes—that's nearly 20% of the database that was just... wrong. Outdated. Pointing at people who'd left months or years ago.

We validated who still worked at their companies. Identified duplicate contacts appearing under multiple accounts. And we made sure we were surfacing the right people—IT decision-makers across C-Suite, VP, Director, Manager, and Architect levels.

Not just email addresses. Real, verified intel.

**Hierarchy Mapping:**

Mapped those 305 subsidiaries to 122 parent companies. Identified global headquarters. Connected regional entities.

Basically, we gave them an organizational chart so they'd stop looking like amateurs when they pitched a subsidiary and had no idea it was owned by a company they were already talking to.

**Duplicate Detection:**

Flagged exact duplicates. Identified fuzzy matches. Cleaned up years of messy data entry so their CRM could finally do what it's supposed to do.
