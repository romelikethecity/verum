Why People Look for ZoomInfo Alternatives

ZoomInfo is the market leader for a reason: massive database, sophisticated intent signals, comprehensive features. But that comes with tradeoffs:

  • Price. Annual contracts typically start at $15,000‑25,000 and scale to $50,000+ for larger teams. That's a significant commitment before you know if it works for you.
  • Complexity. ZoomInfo is a platform you need to learn and manage. Many teams need a dedicated admin to get full value.
  • Data decay. Even ZoomInfo's data decays at 20‑30% annually. Having access to millions of records doesn't help if a third are outdated.
  • Not a cleaning solution. ZoomInfo adds new data but doesn't fix duplicates, validate existing contacts, or clean your CRM. More data on top of bad data makes things worse.

Verum isn't a ZoomInfo competitor in the traditional sense—we don't offer a self-serve database. Instead, we're a done-for-you data service: you send us your data, we clean and enrich it, you get accurate results back.

Key differences from ZoomInfo:

Consider Verum if: Your CRM is full of duplicates, outdated contacts, and inconsistent data. ZoomInfo adds records; Verum fixes your database.

2. Apollo.io

Best for: Budget-conscious teams needing prospecting with built-in outreach.

Apollo offers a compelling value proposition: extensive database plus email sequencing at a fraction of ZoomInfo's price. Their free tier is genuinely useful, and paid plans start at $49/user/month.

Pros: Affordable, includes outreach tools, large database (250M+ contacts)

Cons: Data quality varies, no data cleaning, still requires your time to build lists

3. Cognism

Best for: Teams selling into Europe or prioritizing phone outreach.

Cognism built their reputation on GDPR-compliant European data and verified mobile numbers (their "Diamond Data" program). For teams doing significant phone outreach in EMEA, they're often the better choice.

Pros: Strong European data, human-verified phone numbers, GDPR compliant

Cons: Expensive ($15K+ annually), weaker US coverage than ZoomInfo, no data cleaning

4. Lusha

Best for: Individual contributors doing quick LinkedIn lookups.

Lusha's browser extension makes contact lookup simple: see a LinkedIn profile, click, get email and phone. For SDRs doing manual prospecting, it's convenient and affordable.

Pros: Easy to use, free tier available, includes phone numbers

Cons: Per-lookup model gets expensive at scale, not for batch processing, no cleaning

5. Clearbit (now HubSpot)

Best for: Product-led companies needing real-time form enrichment.

Clearbit pioneered API-first enrichment—instantly appending firmographic data when someone fills out a form. Now part of HubSpot, it's particularly valuable for PLG companies optimizing conversion flows.

Pros: Real-time enrichment, strong APIs, HubSpot integration

Cons: Requires technical implementation, not for batch cleaning, becoming HubSpot-centric

6. LinkedIn Sales Navigator

Best for: Relationship-based selling where LinkedIn presence matters.

Sales Navigator isn't a data provider—you can't export contacts. But for teams doing LinkedIn-centric outreach, the advanced search and InMail features are valuable at $79‑139/month.

Pros: Direct LinkedIn access, relationship intelligence, reasonable price

Cons: No data export, no email addresses, limited to LinkedIn users

7. Seamless.AI

Best for: Teams wanting real-time contact verification.

Seamless.AI verifies contact data in real-time as you search, claiming higher accuracy than static databases. Pricing is credit-based with plans starting around $147/month.

Pros: Real-time verification, Chrome extension, integrations

Cons: Credit model can get expensive, aggressive sales tactics reported, mixed accuracy reviews

The Hidden Problem: More Data ≠ Better Data

Most ZoomInfo alternatives focus on the same thing ZoomInfo does: giving you access to more contacts. But if your CRM already has thousands of records—many duplicated, outdated, or incomplete—adding more data makes the problem worse, not better.

Before switching data providers, consider whether you have a data acquisition problem or a data quality problem:

  • Data acquisition problem: "We need more contacts to reach." → ZoomInfo alternatives like Apollo or Cognism help.
  • Data quality problem: "Our CRM is a mess—duplicates, bounces, outdated info." → You need data cleaning first. That's what Verum does.

Many teams discover that cleaning their existing data is more valuable than adding more records. A smaller, accurate database outperforms a larger, messy one.
