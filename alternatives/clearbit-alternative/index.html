Why People Look for Clearbit Alternatives

Clearbit built an excellent product for real-time data enrichment. But the HubSpot acquisition and the API-first model create situations where alternatives make sense:

  • HubSpot acquisition concerns. Clearbit is becoming increasingly HubSpot-centric. If you're on Salesforce, Pipedrive, or another CRM, the integration story may weaken over time.
  • Technical overhead. Clearbit's power comes from APIs. That requires developers to implement and maintain integrations—overhead not every team can support.
  • Real-time vs. batch. Clearbit excels at enriching forms in real-time. But if you need to clean and enrich thousands of existing records, real-time APIs are the wrong tool.
  • Pricing at scale. Clearbit charges per API call or by monthly volume. For batch enrichment of large databases, this gets expensive quickly.

2. Apollo.io

Best for: Teams wanting enrichment plus prospecting and outreach in one platform.

Apollo offers data enrichment alongside their prospecting database and outreach tools. Their enrichment API can handle real-time use cases, though it's not as polished as Clearbit's was.

Pros: All-in-one platform, affordable pricing, good for startups

Cons: Enrichment isn't their focus, data quality varies, still requires technical implementation for API use

3. ZoomInfo

Best for: Enterprise teams needing comprehensive data and enrichment capabilities.

ZoomInfo has enrichment APIs that can replace Clearbit for real-time use cases. Their database is larger, though their APIs are less elegant. Significant price increase from Clearbit.

Pros: Largest database, comprehensive features, strong enrichment

Cons: Expensive ($15K‑50K+ annually), complex platform, requires technical implementation

4. 6sense

Best for: Teams that used Clearbit Reveal for website visitor identification.

If Clearbit Reveal (identifying anonymous website visitors) was your main use case, 6sense is a direct competitor. They focus on intent data and account identification for ABM strategies.

Pros: Strong visitor identification, intent signals, ABM focus

Cons: Enterprise pricing, complex platform, different product than Clearbit's enrichment APIs

5. People Data Labs

Best for: Technical teams building custom data applications.

People Data Labs offers raw data APIs similar to Clearbit's—person and company data you can query programmatically. Good for teams with engineering resources building data-powered products.

Pros: Flexible APIs, competitive pricing, developer-friendly

Cons: Requires significant technical work, raw data needs processing, no turnkey solution

6. Cognism

Best for: Teams needing European data or verified phone numbers.

Cognism isn't a direct Clearbit replacement, but if your enrichment needs include European contacts or phone numbers, their GDPR-compliant data and Diamond Data verified phones are strong.

Pros: Best European data, verified phone numbers, GDPR compliant

Cons: Not API-first like Clearbit, expensive, more prospecting tool than enrichment platform

Real-Time vs. Batch: Know What You Need

Clearbit's strength was real-time enrichment—someone fills out a form with just an email, and instantly you have their full profile. That's powerful for conversion optimization and lead routing.

But many Clearbit customers used it differently: periodic batch enrichment of their database. For that use case, real-time APIs are overkill and expensive. Batch processing is simpler and cheaper.

  • Real-time enrichment: "Enrich leads as they come in via forms." → Apollo, ZoomInfo, or People Data Labs APIs.
  • Batch enrichment: "Enrich our existing database of 10,000 contacts." → Verum's done-for-you service is simpler and often cheaper than API-based approaches.
  • Website visitor ID: "Identify anonymous website visitors." → 6sense or Demandbase, not Clearbit's enrichment APIs.
