Why Teams Look for Clearbit Alternatives

Clearbit built a great product. Real-time enrichment, clean APIs, solid company data. Then HubSpot acquired them, rebranded it as Breeze Intelligence, and the landscape changed. Here's what we hear from former Clearbit users:

  • The HubSpot lock-in problem. Clearbit as a standalone product is gone. Breeze Intelligence is a HubSpot feature now. If you're on Salesforce, Pipedrive, or any other CRM, you've lost your enrichment provider. Even if you are on HubSpot, the integration is tighter but the flexibility is narrower. You're inside HubSpot's ecosystem whether you want to be or not.
  • Enrichment without cleaning. Clearbit was always an enrichment tool, not a data cleaning tool. It could append company data to a contact record, but it couldn't tell you that 15% of your database is duplicates, that 3,000 records have no valid email, or that your industry field has 47 different variations of "healthcare." If your data is messy before enrichment, it's still messy after. Just with more fields.
  • Shallow on owner and decision-maker data. Clearbit excelled at company-level firmographics: employee count, revenue range, tech stack, industry classification. But when you needed to find the actual person who makes buying decisions at a 40-person company, especially outside of tech? Coverage dropped off. Owner data for SMBs and mid-market was consistently thin.
  • Pricing changes with the acquisition. Clearbit's pricing was already opaque (custom quotes for most plans). Under HubSpot, the enrichment credits are bundled into HubSpot tiers, which makes it harder to evaluate the cost of enrichment alone. Some teams that were paying $X for Clearbit standalone are now looking at a larger HubSpot bill for the same functionality.

Common Questions

I'm on HubSpot. Should I just use Breeze Intelligence instead?

If you only need basic company enrichment (employee count, industry, revenue) and you're already paying for a HubSpot tier that includes it, Breeze might cover your needs. But if you need data cleaning, owner identification, or deeper enrichment than HubSpot's built-in credits provide, Verum fills those gaps. Many HubSpot teams use both: Breeze for real-time form enrichment, Verum for bulk cleaning and deeper projects.

Can Verum replicate Clearbit's real-time enrichment API?

No, and we don't try to. If you need instant enrichment the moment a form is submitted, that's a real-time API use case and there are good tools for it. Verum is built for batch operations: cleaning your database, enriching thousands of records, building prospect lists, identifying business owners. Different problem, different solution. Teams that need both often pair Verum with a lightweight real-time tool.

What happens to my data if I stop using Verum?

Nothing. It's yours. Every record we clean or enrich gets delivered to you in your format of choice. It lives in your CRM, your spreadsheet, your data warehouse. There's no license that expires, no deletion clause, no "you must maintain your subscription to keep accessing the data." That was actually one of the cleaner things about old Clearbit too, and we kept the same philosophy.
