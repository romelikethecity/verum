Why Teams Look for Lusha Alternatives

Lusha is a solid tool for what it does: quick contact lookups from LinkedIn profiles. The Chrome extension is fast, the data is decent for common roles at well-known companies, and the UI is simple. But as teams grow or their data needs get more complex, Lusha's limitations become clear.

  • Per-seat pricing gets expensive at scale. Lusha charges per user per month, with credit allocations that vary by plan. For a single SDR doing one-off lookups, the cost is reasonable. But once you have 10, 20, or 50 people on the platform, you're paying seat fees whether those users are active or not. A 15-person sales team on Lusha's Pro plan can easily run $500-$700/month, and that's before anyone runs out of credits.
  • Credits run out fast. Lusha's credit system means every contact reveal costs a credit. Run an aggressive prospecting campaign and your team hits the wall mid-month. Then it's either upgrade the plan, buy credit add-ons, or tell your reps to slow down. None of those are great options when you're trying to hit pipeline targets.
  • Limited to contact lookups. Lusha gives you an email and a phone number for a person you've already found. That's useful, but it's a narrow slice of what most teams actually need. Lusha doesn't clean your existing data, doesn't identify business owners, doesn't deduplicate your CRM, doesn't verify your database at scale. It's a lookup tool, not a data solution.
  • The Chrome extension model doesn't scale. Lusha's workflow is: find someone on LinkedIn, click the extension, get their contact info. That works for individual lookups. But if you need to enrich 10,000 records in your CRM, clean up a messy database after a migration, or build a targeted list of business owners in a specific market, clicking a Chrome extension one record at a time isn't realistic.

Common Questions

Is Verum more expensive than Lusha?

On a per-lookup basis, Lusha can be cheaper for individual contact reveals, especially on their free or lower tiers. But the comparison shifts when you factor in the full picture. Lusha charges per seat plus credits, so a 10-person team paying $50/user/month plus credit overages can easily spend $600+/month. Verum charges per record with no seat fees, so a one-time project to clean and enrich 5,000 records might cost a similar amount but covers your entire database in one shot. The better question is: what are you actually trying to accomplish?

Can I use Verum for one-off lookups like Lusha?

Verum isn't designed for real-time, one-at-a-time lookups. If you need to quickly grab a phone number before a cold call, Lusha or a similar Chrome extension is faster for that use case. Verum is built for batch work: cleaning databases, enriching contact lists, identifying business owners across hundreds or thousands of records. Some teams use both: Lusha for in-the-moment lookups and Verum for periodic database maintenance and bulk projects.

What if I only need to enrich a few hundred records?

That's fine. There's no minimum order size. If you have 300 records that need cleaning and enrichment, we'll process 300 records. You won't be pushed into a larger commitment or a plan tier you don't need. Per-record pricing means the scope is whatever you need it to be.
