Why Teams Look for Cognism Alternatives

Cognism has built a strong reputation, especially in EMEA. Their phone-verified mobile data for European contacts is genuinely best-in-class. But not every team selling B2B needs a platform optimized for calling prospects in London and Frankfurt. Here's what we hear from teams considering a switch:

  • EMEA-first coverage doesn't match a US-focused GTM. Cognism's biggest differentiator is Diamond Data: human-verified European mobile numbers. If your sales team is calling into North America, that's a premium you're paying for a feature that doesn't help you. US coverage exists in Cognism's database, but it's not where the product shines.
  • Annual contracts lock you in. Cognism requires annual commitments, typically starting around $15,000-$25,000/year. For growing teams or companies with seasonal sales cycles, that's a big commitment before you've validated whether the data actually converts for your market.
  • It's still a self-serve platform. Cognism gives you access to their database and tools, but someone on your team needs to build lists, manage filters, export data, and QA the results. If you don't have a dedicated data ops person, the platform ends up underused.
  • Data cleaning isn't part of the deal. Cognism helps you find new contacts. But if your existing CRM is full of duplicates, outdated titles, and bad emails, that's a separate problem you're left to solve on your own. Layering new contacts onto a messy database just makes the mess bigger.

Cognism is a good product for the right buyer. If you're running outbound across Europe and need verified mobile numbers for EMEA decision-makers, it's hard to beat. But if you sell primarily in the US and want someone to handle data quality for you, there's a different approach.
