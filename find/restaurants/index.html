Who Sells to Restaurants

Restaurants are one of the most actively targeted B2B markets. They buy constantly, they're easy to identify, and there are hundreds of thousands of them. The challenge is reaching the right decision-maker at the right time with the right offer.

Food and beverage. Broadline distributors, specialty food suppliers, beverage companies, local farms. Every restaurant buys food, and most are open to trying new suppliers if the quality and price are right.

Equipment and supplies. Kitchen equipment, smallwares, furniture, tabletop, cleaning supplies, packaging. Restaurants replace and upgrade constantly. New restaurants need everything.

Technology. Point of sale, online ordering, inventory management, scheduling, reservation systems. Restaurant tech is one of the fastest-growing B2B categories. Owners and operators are the buyers.

Financial services. Payment processing, working capital, equipment financing, payroll services. Restaurants have high transaction volume and often need cash flow solutions.

Marketing. Third-party delivery, loyalty programs, local advertising, social media management, review platforms. Restaurants need customers, and marketing vendors help them get them.

Services. Pest control, grease trap cleaning, equipment maintenance, linen service. Restaurants have ongoing service needs that create recurring revenue for vendors.

Restaurant Segments

Full-service restaurants. Table service, tipping. Fine dining to casual. Higher check averages, more complex operations.

Quick-service restaurants. Counter service, drive-through. Fast food, pizza delivery. High volume, standardized operations.

Fast casual. Higher quality than QSR, no table service. Fastest-growing segment. Often younger brands with modern tech stacks.

Bars and taverns. Beverage-focused with food. Different licensing, different vendors, different hours.

Catering and events. Off-premise food service. Different equipment, different sales cycle, often B2B customers.

Ghost kitchens. Delivery-only operations. No front of house. Growing rapidly since 2020.

Common Questions

What types of food service can you find?

Full-service, quick-service, fast casual, bars, cafes, catering, food trucks, ghost kitchens, institutional food service. If it serves food, we can find it.

Who are the decision-makers?

Depends what you're selling. Owners for strategic decisions. GMs for operations. Kitchen managers/chefs for food and equipment. Multi-unit operations may have corporate contacts.

Franchises vs independents?

We can filter either way. Each has different purchasing authority. Franchises may have some mandates from the brand. Independents decide everything themselves.

How current is the data?

Restaurants turn over frequently. We verify using multiple sources including health permits, which require regular renewal. Lists reflect currently operating establishments.
