Healthcare Business Types We Cover

Healthcare is more than doctor's offices and hospitals. There's a vast ecosystem of healthcare businesses, many of them entrepreneurial operations where owners make all the decisions. Different segments have different needs, but they all need vendors.

Home health and hospice. Home health agencies, hospice providers, private duty nursing. Heavily regulated, often owner-operated. Owners deal with staffing challenges, billing complexity, and compliance requirements.

DME and medical supplies. Durable medical equipment suppliers. Wheelchairs, oxygen, hospital beds, prosthetics. Retail and service operations with complex billing. Owners manage inventory, delivery, and compliance.

Labs and diagnostics. Clinical laboratories, blood draw centers, imaging facilities. Heavy capital investment, technical operations, complex billing. Owners are often pathologists, radiologists, or healthcare entrepreneurs.

Urgent care and retail health. Walk-in clinics, occupational health, retail clinics. Growing segment with many independent operators alongside national chains.

Ambulatory surgery centers. Outpatient surgical facilities. Often physician-owned, sometimes joint ventures with health systems. High-margin, equipment-intensive operations.

Behavioral health. Mental health centers, addiction treatment, psychiatric facilities. Growing demand, staffing challenges, reimbursement complexity. Mix of independent and PE-backed operators.

Therapy and rehabilitation. Physical therapy, occupational therapy, speech therapy. Often owner-operated practices. Growing demand, labor-intensive operations.

Healthcare Business Segments

By business type. Each healthcare segment has different regulatory requirements, reimbursement models, and vendor needs. Home health is different from DME is different from labs.

By ownership. Independent owner-operators make quick decisions. PE-backed businesses have portfolio company processes. Health system affiliates have corporate oversight.

By size. Single-location operations vs multi-site operators. Size affects purchasing process, budget, and vendor relationships.

By geography. State licensing, payer contracts, and referral networks are often local. Healthcare businesses work defined geographic areas.

By payer mix. Medicare-heavy, Medicaid-heavy, commercial-heavy, or cash-pay. Payer mix affects revenue cycle needs and cash flow.

Common Questions

What types of healthcare businesses?

Home health, DME, labs, urgent care, ASCs, behavioral health, therapy practices, imaging centers, hospice, skilled nursing, and more.

Owners vs administrators?

Business registrations and CMS data identify ownership. Administrators run operations but don't own. We can provide both.

PE-backed vs independent?

We can filter. PE-backed businesses have different decision processes. Independent operators decide faster. Depends what you need.

NPI and licensing data?

Yes. Healthcare businesses that bill Medicare/Medicaid have NPI. Many also have state licenses. We can include this data.
