Who Sells to Healthcare Businesses

Healthcare is the largest industry in the US economy. Businesses that serve healthcare providers range from medical equipment to billing software to office supplies. Different products reach different decision-makers.

Medical equipment and supplies. Clinical equipment, exam tables, diagnostic devices, disposable supplies. Physicians and office managers make these decisions at independent practices. Procurement departments handle it at larger organizations.

Practice management software. EHR, scheduling, billing, patient communication. Technology decisions are made by physicians, office managers, or IT directors depending on practice size. These purchases are sticky once made.

Revenue cycle management. Billing services, coding assistance, claims management, denial recovery. Practice administrators control these decisions. The pain of managing revenue cycle in-house drives outsourcing.

Staffing and recruiting. Healthcare has chronic staffing shortages. Nurses, medical assistants, administrative staff. Staffing agencies target practice managers and HR leaders at larger organizations.

Marketing services. Patient acquisition, reputation management, website development, SEO. The physician owner or practice administrator decides. Most practices know they should do more marketing.

Compliance and credentialing. HIPAA compliance, credentialing services, regulatory consulting. Required activities that practices often outsource.

Financial services. Practice loans, equipment financing, merchant services, retirement plans. Independent practice owners need these just like other business owners.

Healthcare Business Segments

Physician practices. Primary care, specialists, multi-specialty groups. Still the core of ambulatory healthcare. Range from solo practitioners to 100+ physician groups.

Dental practices. General dentistry, specialists, DSO-affiliated. Similar structure to physician practices with different specialties.

Urgent care and retail clinics. Walk-in care without appointments. Fast-growing segment with mix of independent operators and chains.

Ancillary services. Imaging centers, labs, physical therapy, home health, DME suppliers. Support the healthcare ecosystem.

Behavioral health. Mental health practices, substance abuse treatment, counseling centers. Growing segment with unique needs.

By ownership. Independent practices (physician-owned), health system affiliates, PE-backed groups, corporate chains. Ownership affects decision-making and procurement.

Common Questions

What types of healthcare businesses?

Physician practices, dental, chiropractic, physical therapy, mental health, urgent care, imaging, surgery centers, home health, labs, pharmacies, DME. Any business providing healthcare services.

Can you provide NPI numbers?

Yes. NPI is available for providers and organizations that bill Medicare/Medicaid. NPPES database is public. We match NPI to business records.

What about HIPAA?

We provide business contact information, not patient data. Business addresses, owner names, and professional contacts aren't protected health information.

Independent vs health system?

We can filter for independent practices, health system affiliates, PE-backed, or all. Ownership structure affects purchasing decisions.
