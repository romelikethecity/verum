Why Owner Contact Info Matters

You've got a list of businesses. Maybe you scraped it, maybe you bought it, maybe you exported it from somewhere. It has company names, generic emails, main phone numbers. And it's not working.

Generic emails go to info@ or contact@ addresses that nobody reads. Main phone numbers reach receptionists who send you to voicemail. Direct mail addressed to "Business Owner" gets thrown away. You're not reaching the person who can say yes.

Owner contact information is different. It's the actual person's name. Their direct email, not the company inbox. Their direct line, not the main number. For direct mail, their name on the envelope instead of "Current Resident" or "Business Owner."

The difference in response rates is dramatic. Personal outreach to a named individual converts 3-5x better than generic company contact. The research investment pays for itself on the first campaign.

How We Find Owner Information

Owner data isn't sitting in a database waiting to be queried. It requires actual research.

Business registrations. Secretary of state filings, corporate registrations, DBA filings. These often list the actual owner or registered agent.

Professional licenses. Many businesses require owner licensing (contractors, healthcare, financial services). License databases are public records.

LinkedIn research. Cross-reference company employees to identify founders, owners, and principals. Verify against other sources.

Website research. About pages, team pages, press releases. Many businesses name their owners publicly.

Email verification. Once we have a probable email, we verify it actually exists and will deliver before including it.
