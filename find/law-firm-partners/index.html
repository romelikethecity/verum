Law Firm Partners

Law firm websites list partners, but they don't list direct emails or tell you who actually manages the budget.

Verum identifies law firm partners and the specific roles they play: managing partner, practice group leader, hiring partner, or of counsel. We deliver direct contact info that gets you past the receptionist and the marketing coordinator who screens vendor emails.

The Problem

Law firm partner directories are easy to find on firm websites. Getting their direct email address and phone number is the hard part. Most firms route everything through a main number and generic email. Partners at Am Law 100 firms are especially insulated behind layers of staff.

How We Find Law Firm Partner Contacts

  • State bar association records. Active bar membership records confirm partner status, admission dates, practice areas, and disciplinary history across all jurisdictions where they're licensed.
  • Firm website and bio scraping. We pull partner names, practice areas, education, and notable matters from firm websites, then match this against our contact databases.
  • Role and authority identification. We determine whether each partner is a managing partner (budget authority), practice group leader (hiring authority), or equity vs. non-equity partner (investment authority).
  • Direct email pattern analysis. Law firms use predictable email formats. We identify the pattern for each firm and verify individual partner emails through deliverability testing.
  • Direct dial and mobile discovery. We find direct office extensions and personal mobile numbers that bypass the firm's main switchboard.

What Your Law Firm Partner List Includes

  • Partner name and role. Managing partner, practice group head, hiring partner, equity partner, or of counsel, so you know who to pitch what.
  • Direct email and phone. Verified personal email at the firm domain and direct dial extension or mobile number.
  • Practice areas. Litigation, corporate/M&A, real estate, IP, labor and employment, tax, and other specialties from bar records and firm bio data.
  • Bar admissions. Which states they're admitted in, admission year, and bar number for each jurisdiction.
  • Firm details. Firm name, size tier (Am Law 100/200, midsize, boutique, solo), and office location for that partner.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you filter law firm partners by practice area?

Yes. We tag partners by practice area using bar association records and firm bio data. You can filter for litigation partners, corporate/M&A, IP, real estate, labor and employment, tax, healthcare, or any other specialty. This is useful if your product specifically serves one practice area.

How do you find managing partners versus other partners?

Managing partners are identified through firm websites, legal directories (Martindale-Hubbell, Chambers), and press releases. We also flag practice group leaders and hiring partners, since each role controls different budgets and decisions.

Can you build lists of partners at specific firm sizes?

Yes. We classify firms by size: Am Law 100, Am Law 200, midsize (20-100 attorneys), small (5-20), and boutique/solo. Your selling motion to a BigLaw managing partner is different from a small firm owner, and our lists reflect that.
