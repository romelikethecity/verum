Why Target Partners Specifically

Law firm partners are the decision-makers. Associates work at the firm; partners own it. When you're selling to law firms, partners approve the purchases, sign the contracts, and allocate the budget. Reaching partners directly cuts through the gatekeepers.

Practice management and technology. Case management software, document management, time tracking, billing systems. Managing partners and IT committees evaluate these. Major technology decisions require partner approval.

Marketing and business development. Websites, SEO, thought leadership, client development coaching. Partners control marketing budgets and strategy. They decide what the firm invests in to attract clients.

Professional services. Accounting, HR, recruiting, real estate. Partners make these decisions for the firm. Office managers might implement, but partners approve.

Financial services. Banking, lines of credit, E&O insurance, retirement plans. Partners are the principals who sign and guarantee. Financial vendors need to reach them directly.

Legal services vendors. Research tools, court reporting, process serving, expert witnesses. Partners influence these choices even when associates use the services.

Partner Segments

By role. Managing partners run the business. Practice group leaders head departments. Rainmakers bring in clients. Each has different priorities and purchasing authority.

By firm size. Solo practitioners are their own partner and decide everything. Small firm partners share decisions. Large firm partners work through committees.

By practice area. Corporate partners have different vendor needs than personal injury partners. Employment lawyers buy different services than IP attorneys.

By firm type. Plaintiff firms, defense firms, transactional practices, litigation boutiques. Business model affects purchasing patterns.

By geography. Local practices vs regional vs national firms. AmLaw 100 vs mid-market vs boutiques. Size and geographic scope affect needs and budgets.

Common Questions

Managing partners run the business side. Practice group leaders head departments. Equity partners have ownership. We can filter by partner type.
