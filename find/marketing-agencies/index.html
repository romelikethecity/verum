Who Sells to Marketing Agencies

Marketing agencies are attractive customers. They buy tools, outsource production, and influence their clients' technology decisions. Getting to agency owners often means getting to their clients too.

Software platforms. Project management, creative tools, analytics, client reporting, social media management. Agencies are early adopters. They try new tools and recommend them to clients.

White-label services. SEO fulfillment, paid media management, content writing, web development. Agencies resell these services to clients. They need reliable partners who can deliver consistently.

Media and advertising. Publishers, ad networks, sponsorship opportunities. Agencies control media budgets and need inventory options for their clients.

Business services. Accounting, legal, HR, insurance. Agency owners need the same professional services as other small business owners.

Training and consulting. Agency growth consulting, pricing strategy, operations improvement. Many agency owners want to grow but aren't sure how to scale.

Data and research. Market research, competitive intelligence, consumer insights. Agencies need data to inform strategy for clients.

Data Fields Available

  • Agency information. Name, address, phone, website, years in business, estimated headcount and revenue.
  • Owner/principal contacts. Founders, CEOs, managing partners. Direct email and phone when available.
  • Agency specialty. Full-service, digital, creative, PR, SEO, paid media, social, content, branding, development.
  • Industry focus. Healthcare, B2B, e-commerce, SaaS, financial services, hospitality, nonprofit. Agencies often specialize.
  • Size indicators. Employee count, estimated revenue, number of clients, office locations.
  • Technology stack. Marketing platforms used, CMS preferences, analytics tools (when identifiable from websites).

Agency Segments

Full-service agencies. Strategy through execution across channels. Larger teams, bigger clients, broader capabilities.

Digital marketing agencies. SEO, paid search, paid social, email marketing. Performance-focused, metrics-driven.

Creative agencies. Branding, visual identity, campaign creative, video production. Design-led approach.

PR and communications firms. Media relations, crisis communications, influencer management. Relationship-driven.

Specialized boutiques. Social media only, content only, Amazon specialists, HubSpot partners. Deep expertise in narrow areas.

By size. Freelancer/solo, small (2-10), mid-size (11-50), large (50+). Different products fit different sizes.
