Who Sells to Restaurant Owners

Restaurants are purchasing machines. They buy food, equipment, supplies, technology, and services constantly. They also have notoriously thin margins, which means they're price-sensitive but also always looking for ways to reduce costs or increase revenue.

Food and beverage distributors. Sysco and US Foods have much of the market, but there's room for specialty distributors, local suppliers, and niche products. Owners and kitchen managers make these decisions.

Restaurant equipment. Ovens, refrigeration, smallwares, furniture, signage. New restaurants need everything. Existing restaurants replace and upgrade. Equipment companies sell to owners and operators.

Technology. POS systems, online ordering, reservation systems, inventory management, kitchen display systems. Restaurant tech is booming. Owners make these decisions, sometimes with input from managers.

Marketing and delivery. Third-party delivery, loyalty programs, social media marketing, review management. Restaurants need customers, and they're willing to pay for tools that drive traffic.

Financial services. Merchant processing, working capital loans, equipment financing. Restaurants have high transaction volume and cash flow challenges. Financial services vendors target them heavily.

Staffing and HR. Restaurant turnover runs 75%+ annually. Staffing agencies, HR software, and training companies have a built-in market.

Restaurant Segments

By service type. Full-service restaurants (table service) vs quick-service (counter service) vs fast casual (quick with higher quality). Very different operations, different needs.

By concept. Fine dining, casual dining, family restaurants, bars and taverns, cafes, catering operations, food trucks. Each has distinct purchasing patterns.

By ownership. Independent operators make all decisions. Franchisees have some purchasing mandates and some flexibility. Corporate restaurants centralize most purchasing.

By scale. Single-location independents, small groups (2-5 locations), regional operators (6-20), large franchisees (20+). Multi-unit operators are high-value targets.

By geography. Urban, suburban, rural. Tourist areas vs residential neighborhoods. Location type affects menu, pricing, and vendor needs.

Yes. Full-service, quick service, fast casual, bars, cafes, food trucks. Also by cuisine: Italian, Mexican, Asian, pizza, seafood, and more.

Multi-unit operators?

High-value targets. We can identify operators with 2-5, 5-20, or 20+ locations. Bigger budgets, standardized purchasing across locations.
