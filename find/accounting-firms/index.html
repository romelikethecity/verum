Data Fields Available

  • Firm information. Name, address, phone, website, years in business, firm type (sole practitioner, partnership, corporation).
  • Owner/partner contacts. Name, title, direct email, direct phone when available. Multiple partners at larger firms.
  • Practice specialties. Tax preparation, audit, advisory, forensic, valuation. Industry focus: healthcare, real estate, nonprofit, etc.
  • Client focus. Individuals, small businesses, mid-market, enterprise. Some firms serve multiple segments.
  • Firm size indicators. Number of CPAs, total staff, estimated revenue. Helps with segmentation and prioritization.
  • Technology indicators. Current software used (when identifiable), website technology stack.

How We Build Accounting Firm Lists

We don't just query a database. We actually research and verify.

Primary sources. State boards of accountancy (CPA license registrations), AICPA member directories, state CPA society rosters. These are the authoritative sources for who's actually licensed and practicing.

Business data. Secretary of state filings, business registrations, professional listings. Helps us identify firm structure and ownership.

Firm research. Websites, LinkedIn, industry directories. We find email patterns, direct contacts, practice areas.

Verification. Every email gets deliverability verification. Phone numbers validated. Owner names confirmed against firm websites and LinkedIn.
