Who Sells to Manufacturers

Manufacturing is a massive, diverse sector. Companies that make things need equipment, materials, services, and software. Different products reach different people within manufacturing organizations.

Industrial equipment and machinery. CNC machines, assembly equipment, material handling, packaging equipment. Plant managers and operations directors evaluate these. Long sales cycles, large purchases.

Raw materials and components. Metals, plastics, chemicals, electronic components. Purchasing managers control these relationships. Volume-based, specification-driven.

MRO supplies. Maintenance, repair, and operations supplies. Lubricants, safety equipment, tools, consumables. Purchasing managers and maintenance supervisors buy regularly.

Software. ERP, MES, quality management, inventory control, maintenance management. Operations leaders and IT directors evaluate. Implementation is complex.

Workforce services. Staffing, training, HR, safety consulting. Manufacturing has labor challenges. HR managers and plant managers need reliable workforce solutions.

Professional services. Engineering, consulting, automation, lean manufacturing, quality certification. Plant managers and executives bring in outside expertise.

Financial services. Equipment financing, working capital, insurance. Owners and CFOs handle these decisions.

Manufacturing Segments

By industry. Food processing, metal fabrication, plastics/rubber, electronics, machinery, chemicals, automotive, aerospace, medical devices, consumer products. Each has different needs.

By size. Small shops (under 50 employees), mid-size (50-500), large (500+). Size affects purchasing process and budget.

Job shops vs OEMs. Contract manufacturers serve multiple customers with custom work. OEMs make their own branded products. Different business models, different priorities.

By certifications. ISO 9001, AS9100 (aerospace), IATF 16949 (automotive), FDA registered, ISO 13485 (medical). Certifications indicate capabilities and markets served.

By geography. Manufacturing clusters in certain regions. Midwest for heavy industry, Southeast for automotive, California for electronics. Location affects logistics and labor.
