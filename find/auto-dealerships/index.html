Who Sells to Auto Dealerships

Dealerships are complex businesses with multiple profit centers. Different products sell to different people within the same dealership.

DMS and software vendors. Dealer management systems, CRM, inventory management, digital retailing tools. The dealer principal or GM makes these decisions.

F&I products. Extended warranties, GAP insurance, paint protection, service contracts. The F&I manager is your buyer.

Marketing services. Digital advertising, website providers, SEO, reputation management, lead generation. Often handled by a marketing manager or GM.

Fixed operations. Service equipment, parts suppliers, shop supplies, diagnostic tools. Service and parts managers control these budgets.

Facility and operations. Signage, furniture, cleaning services, security, utilities. Operations managers or the dealer principal.

HR and training. Sales training, compliance training, recruiting services. Dealerships have chronic turnover and always need talent.

Dealership Segments

Franchise new car dealers. Authorized by manufacturers to sell new vehicles. Higher volume, larger operations, more structured.

Independent used car dealers. No manufacturer affiliation. Range from small lots to large used-car superstores.

Dealer groups. Multi-rooftop operations under common ownership. Centralized decision-making for some purchases.

By brand. Domestic (Ford, GM, Chrysler), import (Toyota, Honda, Nissan), luxury (BMW, Mercedes, Lexus). Different profiles and budgets.

By size. Small (under $10M), mid-size ($10-50M), large ($50M+). Volume affects purchasing patterns.
