Who Sells to Construction Companies

Construction is a massive industry with a wide vendor ecosystem. Different products reach different people within construction companies.

Building materials and supplies. Lumber, concrete, steel, finishes. Purchasing managers and project managers make these decisions.

Equipment and tools. Heavy equipment, power tools, safety equipment. Owners, operations managers, and fleet managers.

Software. Project management, estimating, accounting, time tracking. The owner or operations manager at smaller contractors.

Financial services. Bonding, insurance, equipment financing, lines of credit. Owners and CFOs need these products.

Fleet and vehicles. Trucks, trailers, fleet management. Operations managers control these decisions.

HR and staffing. Construction has chronic labor shortages. Recruiting services and staffing agencies target owners and HR managers.

Safety and compliance. Training, certifications, safety equipment. Safety directors at larger firms, owners at smaller ones.

Contractor Segments

General contractors. Manage entire projects, coordinate subcontractors. Decision-makers for project-wide purchases.

Specialty trade contractors. Electrical, plumbing, HVAC, concrete, framing, roofing. Often work as subs but may also take direct contracts.

By project type. Residential builders (custom, production, remodel), commercial contractors, industrial specialists, infrastructure contractors.

By size. Small (1-10 employees), mid-size (11-50), large (50+). Enterprise (100+) contractors have corporate structures.

By geography. Local contractors, regional operators, national firms. Different reach means different sales processes.

Common Questions

Can you separate GCs from subs?

Yes. General contractors and specialty trades are different markets. Filter by contractor type, trade specialty, project focus, and license classification.

How do you find licensed contractors?

State contractor licensing boards are primary sources. We cross-reference with business registrations and industry associations for complete profiles.

What contacts are available?

Owner, project managers, estimators, operations managers, safety directors. Small contractors are owner-run. Larger companies have departmental decision-makers.

Can you filter by project type?

Yes. Residential, commercial, industrial, infrastructure, renovation, new construction. Also by typical project size ($1M, $1-10M, $10M+).
