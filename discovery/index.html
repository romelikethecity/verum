How We Find Prospects

  • Google Places scanning. New businesses show up on Google before they hit commercial databases. We scan continuously.
  • Business registry monitoring. New incorporations, DBA filings, license applications. Public records that signal new businesses.
  • Industry-specific sources. NPI Registry for healthcare, SEC filings for finance, permit data for construction. Sources specific to your vertical.
  • Web presence detection. New websites, new LinkedIn company pages, new social profiles. Digital footprints of new businesses.
  • Hiring signals. Companies posting jobs in your target role often need your product. Hiring is a buying signal.

What You Get

Every discovery project delivers a ready-to-use prospect list:

  • Business information. Company name, address, phone, website, industry classification.
  • Contact information. Key decision-maker names with LinkedIn profiles where available.
  • Enrichment data. Firmographic details like employee count, revenue range, and specialty.
  • Newness scoring. How recently was this business identified? Newer often means better opportunity.
  • Source attribution. Where did we find each prospect? Useful for tracking which methods work best.

One-Time vs Ongoing

One-time discovery makes sense for market entry, campaign planning, or annual territory refresh. We deliver a complete prospect list for your target criteria.

Ongoing discovery makes sense when you need a steady flow of new prospects. We scan weekly or monthly and deliver new finds as they appear. See our Ongoing Maintenance service.
