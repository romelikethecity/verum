How We Detect New Businesses

  • Google Places monitoring. New businesses create Google listings before anything else. We scan daily.
  • Business registry scanning. New incorporations, LLC filings, DBAs. Public records that signal new businesses.
  • Permit and license data. Building permits, occupancy certificates, professional licenses. Early indicators of new businesses opening.
  • Web presence detection. New domains, new company LinkedIn pages, new social profiles. Digital footprints of startups.
  • Industry-specific sources. NPI Registry for healthcare, SEC filings for finance. Sources specific to your vertical.

What You Get

Every detected business is validated and enriched before delivery:

  • Business name and address. Verified against multiple sources.
  • Phone number. Validated as active and correctly associated with the business.
  • Website. When available, including domain age for freshness confirmation.
  • Industry classification. Categorized to match your targeting criteria.
  • Key contacts. Owner or manager names with LinkedIn profiles where available.
  • Detection date. When we first identified this business. Track freshness.

Common Questions

How new are the businesses?

We catch businesses within 1-4 weeks of opening, depending on the source. Google Places is fastest. Business registries can lag a few weeks.

Can you filter by industry?

Yes. We detect based on your target criteria: industry, business type, geography. You don't get a firehose of everything—just prospects that match your ICP.

How do you avoid duplicates with my existing database?

We can compare detected businesses against your current database and only deliver net-new prospects.

What about businesses that open then close quickly?

It happens. We validate before delivery, but some new businesses fail. The upside of reaching good prospects early outweighs the occasional false start.
