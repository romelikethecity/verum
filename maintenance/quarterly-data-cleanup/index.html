The Annual Cleanup Is Too Late

Most companies do a data cleanup once a year. It's a big project. It takes weeks. Everyone's relieved when it's done.

Three months later, 7-8% of the data is already stale again. Six months later, it's 15%. By the time the next annual cleanup rolls around, you've been working bad data for months.

Quarterly cleanup breaks this cycle. Every 90 days, your entire database gets a comprehensive audit. Problems don't compound. Data drift doesn't accumulate. The cleanup never becomes an emergency project.

What's Included in Each Quarterly Cleanup

  • Full validation pass. Every record checked—email deliverability, phone connectivity, business status. Not just active records; the whole database.
  • Deduplication audit. Identify duplicate records across contacts, accounts, and leads. Merge candidates with confidence scores and recommended winners.
  • Enrichment refresh. Re-attempt enrichment on incomplete records. Fill gaps that couldn't be filled last quarter with new data sources.
  • Standardization check. Ensure formats are consistent—addresses, phone numbers, company names, job titles.
  • Health report. Complete metrics on database quality, changes since last quarter, trends, and recommendations.

Why Quarterly?

Quarterly hits the sweet spot between catching problems early and minimizing overhead.

More frequent than annual: You catch problems at the 7-8% decay point, not the 25-30% point. Smaller problems, easier fixes.

Less frequent than weekly: You don't need the overhead of processing changes constantly. Quarterly captures the bulk of decay without constant attention.

Aligned with business cycles: Most companies plan and report quarterly. Data cleanup fits naturally into quarterly planning rhythms.

Quarterly Cleanup vs. Ongoing Monitoring

Quarterly cleanup is comprehensive but periodic. Ongoing monitoring (weekly validation) is continuous but focused.

Many clients use both:

  • Weekly validation catches urgent problems in active records—bad emails before campaigns, dead phones before call blocks.
  • Quarterly cleanup catches everything else—dormant records, duplicates, enrichment gaps, standardization drift.

Together, they provide complete coverage with appropriate frequency for each need.
