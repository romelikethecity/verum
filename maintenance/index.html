The Problem With One-Time Data Projects

You clean your database. It feels great for about three months. Then reality sets in.

Contacts change jobs. Businesses close. Phone numbers get reassigned. Email addresses bounce. The 30% annual decay rate isn't a theory—it's the documented reality of B2B data.

Most companies run a data cleanup project once a year—or worse, once every few years. In between, their database slowly becomes a graveyard of bad records, wasted outreach, and frustrated sales reps.

Ongoing maintenance changes the game. Instead of periodic panic cleanups, your data stays fresh continuously. Problems get caught weekly, not annually.

What Gets Monitored

  • Email deliverability. Continuous validation against bounce lists and deliverability databases. Bad emails get flagged before you send.
  • Phone connectivity. Regular checks for disconnected numbers, reassigned lines, and changed direct dials.
  • Business status. Monitoring for closures, acquisitions, and name changes. No more calling companies that don't exist.
  • Contact currency. Tracking for job changes and departures. Know when your champion moved to a new company.
  • Data completeness. Ongoing enrichment attempts for incomplete records. Fill gaps as new sources become available.

How It Works

Initial setup. We connect to your data source (CRM export, data warehouse, or file delivery) and establish baseline quality metrics.

Weekly monitoring. Automated scans check your active records against validation databases. Issues get flagged immediately.

Monthly reports. Summary of what changed, what degraded, and what got fixed. Clear metrics on database health.

Quarterly deep cleans. Full validation pass, deduplication check, and enrichment refresh. Comprehensive audit with recommendations.

Common Questions

How do you connect to our data?

Most clients send us monthly CRM exports or connect us to their data warehouse. We can also work with file drops (SFTP, cloud storage) or API connections for larger implementations.

Do you write back to our CRM?

We can. For clients who want hands-off maintenance, we deliver updated files that your team imports. For full automation, we can configure direct CRM updates with your approval workflows.

What's the minimum database size?

Maintenance makes sense for databases over 5,000 records. Below that, periodic one-time projects are usually more cost-effective.

Can we pause maintenance temporarily?

Yes. If you're doing a major CRM migration or other project, we can pause and resume. You're not locked into rigid contracts.
