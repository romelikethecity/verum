When Clearbit / Breeze Intelligence Makes Sense

If your entire sales and marketing stack runs on HubSpot, Breeze Intelligence is a natural fit. It lives inside the platform, enriches records automatically as they come in, and doesn't require exporting data anywhere. For HubSpot-native teams that want real-time firmographic enrichment on inbound leads, the tight integration is genuinely useful.

It's also a solid choice if your primary need is company-level data. Clearbit built its reputation on firmographic enrichment: industry, employee count, revenue range, technology stack. If you're doing lead scoring based on company attributes and you're already paying for HubSpot, Breeze Intelligence handles that without adding another vendor.

Teams with developers who want API access will appreciate Clearbit's technical roots. The enrichment API is well-documented and still available for custom integrations, though the standalone product has been increasingly absorbed into HubSpot's ecosystem.

When Verum Is the Better Choice

If you don't use HubSpot, the conversation is basically over. Clearbit is now a HubSpot product. Verum works with Salesforce, HubSpot, Dynamics, Pipedrive, spreadsheets, or whatever you're using. We take data in whatever format you have it and return clean, enriched records you can import anywhere.

Verum is also the better choice when you need more than firmographic enrichment. Clearbit/Breeze fills in company details, but it doesn't clean your data. It doesn't deduplicate records. It doesn't identify business owners or find direct contact information for decision-makers at small businesses. Verum does all of that.

If you're selling into healthcare, local businesses, or SMB markets, Verum's verification pipeline covers ground that Clearbit's company database doesn't. Finding the owner of a three-location medical practice or the decision-maker at a regional service company requires a different approach than matching domains to firmographic data. That's what Verum is built for.

And if your existing data needs serious work before enrichment makes sense. Adding firmographic tags to duplicate records with bad emails and outdated titles just creates a more detailed mess. Verum starts with cleaning because enrichment without clean data is wasted money.

Key Differences

Platform dependency vs. platform freedom. The biggest shift since HubSpot acquired Clearbit is the lock-in. If you're evaluating CRMs, switching from HubSpot, or running a multi-tool stack, Clearbit is no longer an option. Verum is CRM-agnostic by design. Send us a CSV, a Salesforce export, or a database dump. We don't care what system it came from or where it's going.

Enrichment vs. full-service data work. Clearbit enriches records. That's what it does, and it does it well for company-level attributes. But enrichment is one piece of the data puzzle. Verum handles the whole thing: deduplication, standardization, validation, enrichment, and owner identification. For teams that need their data fixed, not just appended to, that distinction matters.

Automated vs. human-verified. Clearbit runs automated enrichment based on domain matching and its proprietary database. Verum combines AI-powered processing with human quality assurance. For high-value records where accuracy matters more than speed, human verification catches things that automated systems miss: outdated titles that match a pattern, businesses that have changed ownership, contacts who've moved to new companies.

The Clearbit you knew might not exist anymore. Clearbit as a standalone product has been gradually folded into HubSpot. Features have been renamed, pricing has changed, and the roadmap is now driven by HubSpot's priorities. If you're comparing based on what Clearbit was in 2022, the current product may not match your expectations.

Common Questions

I already use HubSpot and Breeze Intelligence. Why would I also need Verum?

Breeze enriches records as they enter HubSpot, but it doesn't clean your existing database. If you have years of accumulated contacts with duplicates, outdated information, and missing fields, Breeze won't fix that. Many HubSpot teams use Verum for periodic deep-cleans of their CRM, then let Breeze handle ongoing enrichment for new inbound leads.

Can Verum replace Clearbit for real-time enrichment?

Verum is a project-based service, not a real-time API. If you need instant enrichment the moment a lead fills out a form, that's what Breeze does. Verum is better for bulk projects: cleaning 20,000 records, enriching a purchased list before import, or identifying business owners across your entire database. Different tools for different jobs.

Is Clearbit still available as a standalone product?

The standalone Clearbit product has been largely absorbed into HubSpot as Breeze Intelligence. Some legacy API access may still work for existing customers, but new signups are directed to HubSpot's ecosystem. If you were considering Clearbit specifically because it was CRM-agnostic, that's no longer the case.

What if I'm migrating away from HubSpot?

This is where the lock-in becomes a real issue. If you leave HubSpot, you lose Breeze Intelligence. Your enrichment workflow disappears. Verum's data is delivered as files you own permanently, independent of any platform. Many teams going through CRM migrations use Verum to clean and enrich their data as part of the transition, ensuring they start fresh in the new system with accurate records.
