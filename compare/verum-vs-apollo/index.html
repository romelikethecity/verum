Verum vs Apollo: Understanding the Difference

You're comparing Verum and Apollo. That makes sense on the surface because both companies work with B2B data. But they solve fundamentally different problems, and picking the wrong one means paying for a solution that doesn't address your actual issue.

What Apollo Does Well

Apollo is prospecting and engagement platform. It provides email sequences plus contact data. If you need to build prospecting lists from scratch with net-new contacts you don't currently have, Apollo is designed for that. It's a large database with good coverage.

What {competitor} Doesn't Do

Apollo doesn't clean your existing CRM data. It doesn't deduplicate your records, validate your emails, standardize your fields, or fill in missing firmographic data on accounts you already have. You can import Apollo data, but it enters your CRM alongside your existing mess. Duplicates multiply. Formats conflict.

The Real Question

Is your primary problem finding new contacts (that's Apollo's strength) or fixing the data you already have (that's Verum's strength)? Most teams need both, but if you had to pick one, the answer depends on whether your CRM is empty or dirty.

Price vs. Value

Apollo costs $5K-$24K+/year for platform access. Verum charges per project. If you need ongoing, daily access to a prospecting database, Apollo's subscription model makes sense. If you need periodic data cleanup and enrichment, Verum's per-project model is more cost-effective.
